# SAM-3D-Body/MHR Decode-Fidelity + Playback/Speed Fan-Out — Cited Synthesis

## 1. WHAT CHANGES OUR PLAN

**A. The 262mm defect has a concrete, checkable root-cause path — and our original "diff against a blessed reference" premise was wrong.**
Direct fetch of `MHR/tools/mhr_smpl_conversion/conversion.py::convert_sam3d_output_to_smpl` (repo HEAD, commit `4debaacf`) shows this function **never reads `pred_joint_coords` or `pred_keypoints_3d` at all** — it only operates on `pred_vertices` + `pred_cam_t`. There is no maintainer-blessed formula anywhere for placing the 127-joint MHR skeleton in world/camera space; only the mesh side is specified. **Action: stop looking for a reference skeleton formula; treat "how we place `pred_joint_coords` in world space" as an unsupported extrapolation in our own harness and audit it as such.**

**B. Two specific, testable bugs to check against our harness, both at conversion.py L472-516:**
1. `mhr_vertices = 100.0*pred_vertices + 100.0*pred_cam_t[:,None,:]` — vertices are in **meters**, MHR mesh space is **centimeters**, so *both* terms get the identical 100x scale at the *same point*. If our harness scales vertices but not `cam_t` (or vice versa, or scales at different pipeline stages), that's a tens-of-cm-scale bug — same class as, though larger than, our 262mm outlier.
2. One reconstruction branch does an axis flip (`mhr_vertices[...,[1,2]] *= -1  # Camera system difference in SAM3D-Body`) before adding cam_t; the other branch does not. Getting this backwards on whichever code path we use is an easy, silent bug.
**Action: line-by-line unit/axis-flip check of our decode-and-place code against these two specific lines before concluding anything is "inside the black box."**

**C. `pred_vertices` does *not* carry translation on its own — `pred_cam_t` must be added.** [CORROBORATED, via 2 independent primary-source votes against GH issue #82] Meta contributor JinlongYANG: *"the global translation of those pred_vertices are not included in pred_vertices itself but in pred_cam_t... pred_vertices + pred_cam_t[None, ...]"* (comment dated **2026-01-05**, not 2025-12-05 as originally logged — the issue thread opened 2025-12-05 on an unrelated sub-question). If our harness's "none-vs-field changed nothing" test bypassed or double-applied `cam_t`, that reproduces exactly the observed mesh-vs-skeleton divergence independent of any scale field.

> **Correction — do not act on this as stated:** the claim that reconstruction needs "exactly 4 fields (`mhr_model_params`, `shape_params`, `expr_params`, `pred_vertices`)" with `pred_vertices` as *the* translation-bearing field is **UNVERIFIED** (refuted 1-1, net not-survived). The actual reference code at commit `4debaacf` requires a **5th field, `pred_cam_t`**, unconditionally added in both reconstruction branches — the file's own inline comment states *"Translation is not included in the vertices, but in pred_cam_t."* JinlongYANG's GH comment is a casual simplification that undersells his own code. **Build against the conversion.py source directly, not the paraphrase.**

**D. Possible joint-set mismatch on the "skeleton" side of our divergence metric.** GH issue #34 (opened 2025-11-20, maintainer-resolved 2025-11-23/25): `pred_keypoints_3d` is a *separate, smaller* joint set structurally lacking central spine joints; the full 127-joint skeleton (with spine) lives only in `pred_joint_coords`. **Action: confirm which field our harness reads for the "skeleton" comparator.** If it's `pred_keypoints_3d`, our 262mm worst-joint (plausibly torso/spine, since that's exactly where this gap manifests) may be a field-definition mismatch, not a placement-math bug — a much cheaper fix than continued decode-internals archaeology.

> **Correction:** the bundled claim that issues #60/#92/#65/#34 are "the same class of confusion" is **UNVERIFIED** (refuted 1-1, net not-survived). #34 is unrelated (joint-topology, not scale drift); only #60 actually reports frame-to-frame scale/size instability; dates were padded to span through 2026-06-10 when the latest real comment across all four threads is 2026-01-21. **What does survive as independently re-confirmed against live repo code (2026-07-08 clone):** `mhr_head.py:296` still hardcodes `global_trans = torch.zeros_like(global_rot_euler)`, and `tools/vis_utils.py`'s `visualize_sample_together` still substitutes a heuristic `fake_pred_cam_t` (midpoint of vertex-bbox extremes) instead of real per-person translation for multi-person rendering. Treat these two as real, current code facts — just not as one unified "issue bundle."

**E. Independent, external confirmation this is a known SAM-3D-*family* decode failure mode, not just our harness.** Sibling repo `facebookresearch/sam-3d-objects` issue #57: a real 5cm cube round-tripped through SAM3D decode came out at ~15cm (3x scale error) **even with accurate depth supplied** — "depth pointmap changed nothing" maps directly onto our own "none-vs-field changed nothing." Compounding: issue #69 ("Children are adults, but very far," opened 2025-11-28, still open) shows the decoder's metric-scale mechanism is a **learned body-proportion-to-depth prior**, not a geometric computation — it has no independent metric anchor. And a third-party audit paper [CORROBORATED, 2 votes] — "Investigating Anthropometric Fidelity in SAM 3D Body" (arXiv:2601.06035, 2025-12-02) — independently finds a "regression to the mean" on individual body shape/scale baked into the MHR low-dim representation + DINOv3 conditioning + annotation-based alignment training. **Net: temper how much further "find the exact scale bug" debugging we sink in vs. accepting some residual as an upstream model-family ceiling** to be worked around via constraints rather than chased to zero.

**F. Actionable validation recipe we don't have today.** MetricHMSR (arXiv:2506.09919, v4 2026-06-30) validates metric decode via a **synthetic benchmark with known ground-truth geometry/focal length** rather than a physical rig. **Recommended: build a synthetic render round-trip test** — author a body mesh with known scale/pose/camera, render it through our viewer pipeline, feed the render back into SAM-3D-Body, measure decode error against the ground truth we authored ourselves. This isolates decode-internal fidelity from real-capture noise and is more tractable than a checkerboard/ArUco physical rig (which, per exhaustive search, nobody has built for this model family — GH issue #47, opened 2025-11-21, is still open and unanswered on exactly this question). Bonus: MetricHMSR's own reported errors (PA-MPJPE 33.6mm / MPJPE 53.0mm / PVE 62.7mm on 3DPW) put our 53.5mm p95 in the *same ballpark as normal SAM-3D-Body-family noise* — but our 262mm worst-joint outlier is far outside this range and is what the synthetic test should specifically hunt.

**G. Fixing decode could help the speed target too, not just trade against it.** Fast SAM 3D Body (arXiv:2603.15603, 2026-03) profiles the *same model family's* unoptimized baseline and finds the iterative MHR-to-SMPL fitting/decode step is one of the two dominant wall-time costs (~381ms of the total ~1000ms/frame baseline) — replacing it with a direct feedforward mapping was >10,000x faster for that sub-step alone. **If our decode fix moves toward a more direct/feedforward path (rather than more iteration), it's plausibly speed-positive** for the 6-8min/clip target, not a tax on it. (Caveat: FastHMR arXiv:2510.10868 shows the opposite is true in a *different* architecture where the decoder is nearly free — decode-cost-share is architecture-dependent, so profile our actual stage before assuming either direction.)

**H. Playback/speed: no better mesh compression path is adoptable today, but the field is quietly moving off meshes entirely.** MPEG V-DMC (FDIS July 2025, IS expected March 2026) is purpose-built for exactly our per-frame human mesh sequence problem (base mesh + per-frame displacement, reusing HEVC/VVC hardware codecs) but **has no browser/JS decoder yet** — calendar flag, not actionable now. More consequential: commercial volumetric-video production (DNE/Gracia, April 2026; Spark 2.0 LoD, 2026) is **moving to 4D Gaussian Splatting instead of meshes specifically because meshes flatten soft-tissue/motion detail** — the same class of fidelity loss we're fighting in our 262mm round-trip. This is a legitimate alternative-rendering-target pivot point to flag if decode-fidelity work stalls further, not an immediate action. Cheaper and immediately actionable if/when we serialize to glTF/GLB: **use MeshOpt + `KHR_mesh_quantization`, not Draco** — Draco doesn't compress morph-target-style per-frame-different-vertex-position animation (our exact case) and decodes slower; MeshOpt is what the field has converged on for animated web meshes.

## 2. WHAT CONFIRMS OUR PLAN

- **VERIFIED=0 stance is externally vindicated.** GH issue #47 (SAM-3D-Body metric-accuracy question) is open and unanswered by Meta as of this pass — no one outside Meta has an answer to "is this metrically accurate," so treating nothing as VERIFIED until we build our own gate is the only defensible position.
- **Latent-space smoothing design direction is validated by independent prior art.** [CORROBORATED, 2 votes] "World-Coordinate Human Motion Retargeting via SAM 3D Body" (arXiv:2512.21573, 2025-12-25) builds the closest published precedent to our exact unwired stage: frozen SAM-3D-Body/MHR backbone, **identity/skeleton-scale params locked across a temporal window**, smoothing done **in MHR latent space** (not decoded mesh/joints — confirmed no public prior art does this either, so we're ahead of the field here, not duplicating it), plus a differentiable soft foot-contact model for the killed grounding stage. This is directionally exactly our unwired-smoothing plan, and per-track identity/scale locking is also a candidate *workaround* for the decode-internal scale defect itself (bypass the bug's symptom rather than chase its cause).
- **Fast-SAM-3D-Body NOT-ADOPT ruling holds up against the newer alternatives found.** SAM-Body4D (arXiv:2512.08406, 2025-12-09, same SAM-3D-Body/MHR base) achieves a modest, safe ~2x batching win via track-then-densify masklet conditioning with *no* reported accuracy regression, and TemPoFast3D (arXiv:2505.07333) gets 3.77x speedup via seed-frame-then-propagate with accuracy held (PSNR/SSIM/LPIPS all within tolerance) — both confirm that "speed via smarter frame selection/conditioning" is the safe lever, and "speed via full-model rewrite" (what Fast-SAM-3D-Body did) is the one that broke on our fast-swing case. Our ruling was the right call.
- **grounding_refine being killed and gated on decode is the right sequencing.** OnlineHMR (arXiv:2603.17355, 2026-03) independently frames global/SLAM-style world-grounding optimization as a legitimately heavy, expensive pipeline stage in this problem class generally — not something stance/smoothing dominates. A same-genre physics-refinement paper for fast athletic motion (boxing iLQR, arXiv:2504.08175) shows real promise (foot-float 16.4→8.1mm, foot-slide 2.2→0.3mm with only marginal accuracy cost) but is explicitly downstream of decode and would inherit the 262mm/53.5mm defect — confirms un-kill-after-decode-fix, not before, is correct.

## 3. WATCH ITEMS (no action now, revisit later)

- **SAM-Body4D masklet-conditioning** (arXiv:2512.08406) — cheap eval candidate (batch body+hand crops together) that should be independent of decode fidelity since it operates on prompts/masks, not geometry; worth de-risking in parallel with decode work.
- **TemPoFast3D seed-then-propagate pattern** (arXiv:2505.07333) — different backbone family (SIFU/GTA, not MHR), a pattern to adapt not adopt, once decode is trusted and latent smoothing is un-wired.
- **OnlineHMR** (arXiv:2603.17355, CVPR 2026) — causal/SLAM world-grounding, structurally different fix path than "debug the decode," but full text/numbers unextractable this pass; re-fetch when un-killing grounding_refine.
- **Physics-informed foot-contact/iLQR** (arXiv:2504.08175) — a build-vs-buy candidate for beating our classical foot-pin once decode is fixed; wave-N+ item, not now.
- **Fast SAM 3D Body (arXiv:2603.15603)** — confirm this March-2026 paper is the *same* checkpoint/lineage already benched NOT-ADOPT and not a newer revision worth re-testing; 30-second full-text diff, not done this pass.
- **MPEG V-DMC** — no browser decoder yet; check back after IS ships (~March 2026) against our byte-budget mesh index problem.
- **4DGS web streaming (DNE/Gracia, Spark 2.0)** — possible alternative rendering target if mesh-decode-fidelity work stalls; not urgent.
- **SAM3DBody-cpp reimplementation** — do NOT adopt before our own root-cause is understood; risks masking or duplicating the exact bug we're chasing.
- **MetricHMSR synthetic round-trip methodology** — worth building as our own decode-fidelity gate (see 1.F); no such SAM-3D-Body-specific study exists to just borrow.
