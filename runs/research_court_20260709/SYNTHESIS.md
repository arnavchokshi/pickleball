# Court Auto-Find: Cited Synthesis for Manager Review

Scope: single-frame + video court registration literature 2024–2026, synthetic-to-real domain adaptation at 5–100 real labels, pickleball/racket-sport-specific sources, zero-shot foundation-model composition, and video/temporal calibration — cross-checked against our current truth (court_unet_v2 synthetic-only PCK@5=0.0 on real; line-solver 0/16 on steep/low viewpoints; lock-on and tennis-overlay failures unsolved).

---

## 1. WHAT CHANGES OUR PLAN

**1a. Add a geometric-consistency / quadrilateral-validity loss to the keypoint head — do not retrain blind a third time.**
CourtKeyNet (badminton, ScienceDirect S2666827026000496, 2026) diagnoses our exact failure mode in its own framing: general heatmap keypoint nets "lack domain specific knowledge for correct identification of court borders," producing geometrically invalid or wrong-court predictions. Its fix is architectural, not more data: an Octave Feature Extractor + Polar Transform Attention for boundary sharpening, a hybrid heatmap+regression localization head, a **Quadrilateral Constraint Module** enforcing that predicted corners form a valid quadrilateral, and a **Geometric Consistency Loss** penalizing court-geometry violations during training (source: https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666827026000496, 2026 — paywalled, no PCK/IoU numbers or license extractable; not adversarially refuted, treat architecture idea as directionally sound but unverified numerically). This is a loss/module addition compatible with our existing pipeline — independent of the kill rule on a third synthetic-only retrain, since it changes what the net is penalized for, not what data it sees. **Action: extract the loss formulation from the full paper/preprint before the next court_unet_v3 pass; this is the most direct fix for lock-on (adjacent-identical-court) and geometrically-impossible predictions.**

**1b. Real-data supervision at our scale (500–1,100 images) is externally validated as sufficient for racket-sport court registration when synthetic-only pretraining is dropped.** Jouini/Elloumi/Chaieb (AIAI 2024) trained an encoder-decoder segmentation-to-homography model on 486 real tennis + 564 real badminton YouTube-scraped images (92-image manual IoU test set), explicitly targeting "occluded/faded lines and illumination variations" — our exact steep/low-viewpoint and real-world conditions — and reported outperforming a handcrafted baseline in IoU and inference latency, with no synthetic-only pretraining stage. Adversarial refute confirmed the method, dataset sizes, and outperformance claim as accurate; it corrected only a venue-series detail (published in Springer's **IFIP AICT vol. 713**, not LNCS as originally cited) (DOI 10.1007/978-3-031-63219-8_2; HAL record hal-04622732). Because that correction touched a cited fact, the overall claim is marked UNVERIFIED per protocol, but the substantive method/dataset/result was independently confirmed by both adversarial votes. **Relevance: our ~5 owner-labeled FULL15 frames + projectable frames from 1–3 calibratable sources + the 40 rally clips are in the right order of magnitude for a real-data-first (not synthetic-first) retrain — reinforces pivoting off synthetic-only entirely for the next attempt, using segmentation-mask-to-homography (zone IoU) as an architecture alternative to sparse-point heatmaps, since zones remain partially constraining even when individual corner points are occluded/ambiguous at steep angles.**

**1c. Diffusion-based style adaptation (CACTIF) needs only 1 real image per viewpoint/condition to close synthetic-to-real gap — directly matches our data shape.** [CORROBORATED] CACTI/CACTIF (arXiv 2505.16360, May 2025; code github.com/echigot/cactif) generates 250 diffusion-stylized synthetic variants per target condition from a single real reference image, and on GTA5→ACDC improves mean mIoU by ~4.9pt over a plain source+style baseline (DAFormer 42.80% vs 37.91%; HRDA 45.66% vs 40.74%) and +1.2pt on clean Cityscapes (54.39% vs 53.18%). Every specific number was verified against the primary source. We have far more than 1 image per condition (5 owner-labeled FULL15 frames, 3 calibratable static-tripod sources, 40 rally clips) — enough to stylize our synthetic generator's renders per-source before training. **Action: concrete, low-cost next experiment — restyle synthetic renders with CACTIF conditioned on our 3 calibratable real sources, retrain court_unet_v2→v3 on style-adapted synthetic + geometrically-projected real keypoints (from the calibratable sources), re-gate on PCK@5. This does not violate the "no third synthetic-only retrain" kill rule because the training set is no longer synthetic-only — it's synthetic-with-real-style-conditioning plus projected real supervision.**

**1d. License constraint: BroadTrack's temporal-propagation recipe is not shippable as-is.** BroadTrack (WACV 2025, arXiv 2412.01721) tracks tripod-mounted cameras across video by combining per-frame detection with a tripod/handheld motion model + optical flow + reprojection-error refinement, halving mean reprojection error and gaining >15% Jaccard vs single-frame calibration on SoccerNet, validated stable over 20-minute continuous broadcast (long-horizon drift robustness). This matches our camera setup (static/tripod + light handheld) closely. **However, the GitHub license (github.com/evs-broadcast/BroadTrack) is EVS Broadcast's own non-commercial-research-only agreement — no sublicense, no distribution, no commercial use.** We can study and reimplement the *idea* (tripod-motion-constrained optical-flow-linked frames + joint reprojection optimization across a rally) but cannot vendor their code or weights into DinkVision given the commercial-clean license requirement. **Flag to owner/legal: any BroadTrack-derived component must be a clean-room reimplementation, not a fork.**

**1e. Cheaper near-term temporal lever: Kalman-filter smoothing over per-frame keypoints across a rally clip, no joint bundle adjustment required.** TT3D (table tennis, arXiv 2504.10035, Apr 2025) uses only 4 coplanar corner points/frame (U-Net++ segmentation → Hough-line corner extraction → iterative PnP + focal-length line-search), then a **Kalman filter smooths per-frame focal length and pose across video** to handle camera movement/zoom drift — reporting real-data corner MAE 2.39px ± 1.47px on 40 test images. This is implementable on top of our *existing* per-frame detector output, once it's even weakly informative post-1c, as a second-stage smoother over the 40 rally clips we already have — much cheaper than BroadTrack's joint optimization and license-clean (no commercial restriction found in the TT3D materials cited). **Action: sequence after 1c lands weak-but-nonzero PCK, not before.**

## 2. WHAT CONFIRMS OUR PLAN

**AnyCalib remains the right zero-shot intrinsics prior** — already allowed under our constraints. Confirmed (not adversarially refuted in this pass, but consistent with prior known-names status): AnyCalib (ICCV 2025, Apache-2.0, arXiv 2503.12701) "consistently outperforms alternative methods, including 3D foundation models, despite being trained on orders of magnitude less data." No evidence surfaced that a heavier 3D-foundation-model calibration route (VGGT, Depth Anything-based) beats it — reinforces composing AnyCalib + device/lens profiles rather than chasing a 3D-FM calibration replacement.

**No published low-data (5–100 label) fine-tuning recipe exists for PnLCalib/TVCalib/NBJW-class models.** Their published strength comes from datasets 1–2 orders of magnitude larger than ours (SoccerNet-Calibration, WorldCup2014, TS-WorldCup — thousands of real labeled frames). This tempers any plan to "just fine-tune PnLCalib on our 5–100 real frames" and **confirms our existing plan (geometric-projection pseudo-labeling from the 1–3 calibratable sources) as the more evidence-backed path** to scale real supervision beyond hand-labels, rather than expecting transfer-learning shortcuts the literature doesn't demonstrate.

**Our tennis-overlay dual-line-family synthetic generator family and adjacent-identical-court hard negatives remain ahead of the published literature** — no 2024–2026 source (soccer, tennis, badminton, or racket-sport-generic) describes an explicit "adjacent identical court" or "dual-line-family overlay" disambiguation mechanism. This is a genuine data/product moat, not a solved-elsewhere gap we're behind on, and confirms we should keep investing here rather than searching further for an importable fix.

**Guided/assisted calibration as a fallback is validated by competitor behavior, not just our own product pragmatism.** PlaySight's disclosed calibration patent describes a guided close-up-surface capture step (conceptually parallel to our own guided corner-tap confirmation) rather than a claim of pure auto-find from an arbitrary playing-angle frame; pb.vision markets full auto-calibration from standard mobile footage as their claimed capability but discloses zero method detail; SwingVision's high-accuracy claims are for fixed multi-camera installations, not single handheld phones. None of the three disclose a solved single-shot monocular auto-find — weak but real evidence that even funded competitors either don't have it or don't disclose it, supporting our current guided-fallback ship path while auto-find remains in development.

## 3. WATCH ITEMS

- **CalibAnyView** (2026, arXiv 2605.14615) extends single-view calibration to N≥1 views via a multi-view transformer predicting dense perspective fields + geometric optimization, explicitly reporting "strong robustness under single-view settings, further improves with multi-view inference" — newer than AnyCalib and matches our exact scenario (single static-tripod frame OR multiple rally-clip viewpoints). Not adversarially checked this pass; **follow up for released weights/license before adopting.**

- **SAM 3** (Meta, Nov 2025) adds Promptable Concept Segmentation — text-prompted zero-shot masking of every matching instance (e.g., "court line") in one pass. Could zero-shot-segment court lines/kitchen boundaries on real owner frames with no training, feeding our existing geometric line-solver as a cleaner input than raw Canny/LSD edges. Does not by itself solve lock-on or tennis-overlay ambiguity (still needs a template prior). Worth a cheap bench test against our steep/low-viewpoint failure set.

- **SPIDER** (JHU, arXiv 2511.17750, Nov 2025) combines a 3D-foundation-model backbone with a RoMa-style dense-warp head for robust matching across large viewpoint changes — a genuinely different mechanism than our current pipeline: dense-correspondence matching a real frame directly against a canonical top-down court template, rather than training a heatmap net on synthetic-only data. Speculative (no sports-specific evidence found) but structurally the most direct answer to "zero-shot template correspondence" — worth a bench test on our steep/low-angle failure set before committing engineering time.

- **Depth Anything 3**'s metric-depth formula (`metric_depth = focal * net_output / 300`) uses a known/estimated focal rather than learning scale end-to-end — since we already hold iPhone lens/focal profiles, this is a cheap building block for a court-plane depth regularizer *on top of*, not instead of, the line-solver. Untested by us; flag as a lightweight experiment, not a priority.

- **Sportlight's voter-by-reprojection-RMSE architecture pattern** (arXiv 2410.07401) — score multiple candidate camera-solution hypotheses by minimum reprojection RMSE rather than a fixed rule set — is a reusable idea for our geometric line-solver pool (currently 0/16 on steep/low viewpoints), independent of its soccer-circle-specific geometric machinery. **UNVERIFIED overall per adversarial refute**: the paper's real leaderboard numbers (combined score 0.5535, Acc@5 0.7322, 4.46/4.89px reprojection error, 57-landmark HRNetV2-w48 architecture, Voter algorithm) were independently confirmed by both refute votes, but the claim's characterization of 2nd–4th place finishers as "NBJW/PnLCalib-class baselines" was wrong (they are named challenge teams Spiideo/SAIVA/BPP unrelated to those later papers) and the "pole-polar geometry" framing was not found in the source. **Use the architecture idea; do not cite the mischaracterized baseline comparison or the pole-polar phrasing.**

- **SoccerNet 2025 temporal-propagation-on-GSR pattern** (arXiv 2508.19182) — evidence that the field's 2025 consensus lever is propagating a confident single-frame calibration across video via optical flow/RANSAC rather than re-solving every frame, applicable once we have a confident single-frame solve on any rally-clip frame. **UNVERIFIED overall per adversarial refute**: GSR-13 (NBJW keypoints + geometric-prior auxiliary points + Lucas-Kanade optical flow + RANSAC, +4% over NBJW baseline) and GSR-4 (BroadTrack-style optical-flow propagation) were confirmed correctly attributed; GSR-3 was misattributed to "Constructor.Tech" in the original claim when the primary source credits it to team "lianyou" (Youxing Lian, Tiancheng Wang) — Constructor.Tech submitted no methodology writeup at all. **Cite GSR-13/GSR-4 findings directly; do not attribute the radial-distortion+PnLCalib+[pan,tilt,roll,focal] method to Constructor.Tech.**

- **"Can Geometry Save Central Views for Sports Field Registration?"** (arXiv 2504.20052, Apr 2025) — a non-learned, closed-form method deriving extra point/line correspondences from a single visible conic (circle) via pole-polar geometry, specifically to rescue viewpoints with too few visible field markings, with reported qualitative gains on out-of-distribution low/close-in/sparse-marking views (our exact failure class), though roughly matching baseline in-domain. Not adversarially checked. The circle-specific mechanism doesn't port (pickleball has no circles), but the underlying strategy — derive extra virtual constraints from whatever strong geometric anchor IS visible (e.g., kitchen-line/sideline right-angle junctions, net-post base) rather than requiring the full 15-point set — is a transferable idea for the 0/16 steep/low-viewpoint solver failures. Worth a design spike, not yet an implementation commitment.

- **Roboflow pickle-court-keypoints dataset** (1,135 images, CC BY 4.0, published ~Mar 2025) — a new pickleball-specific court keypoint dataset beyond what we already track; license clears our commercial-clean bar. **Keypoint schema (how many of our 15 metric points it covers) and annotation quality are unverified** — direct fetch returned 403; needs a manual pull before relying on it as a training source.

## 4. DEAD ENDS

- **No zero-shot foundation-model court-geometry system exists with reported PCK/keypoint-accuracy numbers**, for any racket sport or soccer, as of 2025–2026. Every sport-specific field-registration paper found (PnLCalib, NBJW, AuxFlow, KaliCalib, ice-hockey rink registration) is trained/fine-tuned, not zero-shot. The path forward is composing general-purpose primitives ourselves (SAM3 masks + SPIDER/DINOv3 dense correspondence to a canonical template + AnyCalib/CalibAnyView intrinsics + DA3 depth-plane prior) rather than expecting an off-the-shelf drop-in.

- **No academic work addresses tennis-overlay dual-line-family disambiguation or adjacent-identical-court lock-on as a named problem.** Confirms this remains ground we solve ourselves via our own synthetic generator + real data, not importable from the literature.

- **Pickleball is entirely unaddressed at the academic-paper level.** All real pickleball-specific work found is either Roboflow community datasets or undisclosed commercial products (pb.vision, SwingVision, PlaySight) — no paper, no benchmark, no numbers to borrow.

- **No true joint bundle-adjustment-style multi-frame optimization for single-monocular-camera court calibration exists outside BroadTrack**, which is soccer/broadcast/tripod-elevated-camera-specific and non-commercially licensed (see 1d). Nothing pickleball- or generic-racket-sport-specific at that sophistication was found.

- **No sport-court-specific uncertainty/abstention mechanism** ("refuse to calibrate this frame, ask for human confirm") exists in the literature — general object-detection confidence-calibration methods (ECE curves) exist but nothing court-specific. This remains a gap we design ourselves; relevant to our guided-fallback product path (Section 2).

- Several arXiv PDFs (2503.13969 SoccerSynth Field, 2504.20052, ClearLines 2505.00452, HomoFM 2601.18222) could not be text-extracted by the fetch tooling (image-heavy/binary PDFs) — flagged as needing a manual/OCR read before being treated as evidence, not usable as citations yet.
- Multi-camera self-calibration via cross-view human-pose correspondence (arXiv 2604.17567) requires 2+ overlapping cameras — structurally incompatible with our single-iPhone product constraint.
- HomoFM (2026) targets generic two-image homography, not single-frame-to-3D-template sports registration — no evidence it addresses synthetic-to-real transfer; not pursued further.
