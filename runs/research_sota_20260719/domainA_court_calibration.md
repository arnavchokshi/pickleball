44 agents, 85 findings, 10 sources deep-read, 2-vote adversarial refutation. Labels: [CORROBORATED] never means product-VERIFIED.

## Answers to owner questions

### Q1
Q1 — No. We found no published or production system that achieves ~5px court calibration fully automatically (no human framing/GT input) from a single camera, in ANY sport. Best fully-automatic numbers come from soccer (much richer line/circle/goalpost geometry than a pickleball court) and still miss 5px on 40-60% of frames: TVCalib predicted-segmentation AC@5 = 57.6% (SoccerNet) / 41.3% (WorldCup broadcast) [MEASURED, arxiv.org/abs/2207.11709]. The real competitive bake-off (SoccerNet Calibration Challenge 2023, 66 submissions/7 teams) topped out at Accuracy@5=73.22%, Completeness=75.59% [MEASURED, github.com/NikolasEnt/soccernet-calibration-sportlight], and no 2024 edition ran. PnLCalib (2026 SOTA) reaches 80.6-85.9% JaC@5 on easy single-view test sets but drops to 78.4% completeness on harder views and fails outright on close-up/few-landmark frames [MEASURED, arxiv.org/abs/2404.08401]. No pickleball-specific academic work exists at all. Method family doesn't decide the winner either — classical (TVCalib's segment-reprojection optimizer, Farin's original line-fitting) and learned (PnLCalib's HRNet keypoints, KaliCalib) both appear among current leaders; the field's actual state of the art already fuses a learned/classical front-end detector with a classical geometric optimizer back-end, and still doesn't clear ~5px reliably, fully automatically, on data richer than ours.

### Q2
Q2 — Yes, where production systems are single-camera, they calibrate classically against a known court model — SwingVision's patent (US 11,842,572 B2 family) describes color-contrast-based line detection plus Direct Linear Transformation homography fit from LINE correspondences (>=4 lines, not full corner coverage), the same solver family as our own [source: patents.google.com/patent/US11842572B2/en; note: 2 of the narrative claims about this source were flagged CONTESTED/REFUTED by our adversarial pass, so treat the exact patent metadata as directional, the technical method description as solid]. But every single-camera production system we found compensates with imposed user-setup discipline, not solver power: pb.vision requires a static elevated (5ft+) tripod mount, landscape orientation, stabilization/HDR OFF, and mandates all 4 court corners visible with no part cut off, backed by a real-time 'CourtFocus' human-in-the-loop framing guide before recording even starts [MEASURED product requirement, help.pb.vision/articles/1108176]. This directly matches our own frozen-harness finding that pb.vision's 5.67px edge over our 6.61px (<1px apart) is a capture-discipline gap, not a solver gap. SwingVision's patent likewise assumes a fixed courtside mount near the net. Where a single camera and strict setup weren't judged sufficient, systems add MORE cameras instead: Hawk-Eye uses 6+ synchronized fixed cameras triangulating in 3D [topendsports.com/sport/tennis/hawkeye.htm]; SwingVision's own tournament ELC pilot used TWO devices per court, not one [tennis.com/baseline/...]; PlaySight SmartCourt and Zenniz are fixed multi-camera (plus 30 sonar sensors for Zenniz) per-venue installations, not single consumer phones [broadcastnow.co.uk; ca.sports.yahoo.com]. No production system solves single-camera calibration by out-CV-ing the setup problem; they all either constrain the human or add cameras.

### Q3
Q3 — Yes, the general principle is documented precedent in adjacent domains, but no racket-sport paper does exactly our pool-and-lock design, and the two most current sports-calibration SOTA papers explicitly call it unsolved. A generic multi-frame video-stitching paper shows pooling feature-point evidence across a static temporal window (quantized histogram + RANSAC weighted by temporal occurrence) improves reprojection score 23-57% over single-frame and degrades gracefully instead of catastrophically under noise [MEASURED, pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6983053]. PTZ-Calib (2025), the closest analogous production-grade sports system, explicitly fuses evidence across multiple frames of the same fixed-position camera via feature tracking plus incremental+periodic global bundle adjustment rather than per-frame independent solves, and its cheaper incremental variant (0.68s/image vs 29.80s/image for full bundle adjustment) is exactly the 'compute once, reuse' shape we want [MEASURED, arxiv.org/html/2502.09075v1]. BHITK explicitly models homography as evolving frame-to-frame with a Kalman-family filter that retains a forecast through occlusion via propagated covariance [arxiv.org/html/2311.10361v2]. Farin's foundational 2004 method (still cited/reimplemented today) explicitly uses temporal tracking across frames to seed the next frame's optimization for occlusion robustness [dirk-farin.net/publications/data/Farin2004b.pdf]. Crucially, TVCalib (WACV'23) and PnLCalib (2026) — the two most-cited current single-frame sports-calibration SOTA papers — BOTH explicitly list temporal/multi-frame consistency fusion as unaddressed FUTURE WORK in their own text, meaning our planned static-span-pooling design is not something already better-solved elsewhere; it is ahead of the stated frontier on this specific mechanism in the sports-calibration literature, while resting on solid precedent from adjacent video/tracking work.

### Q4
Q4 — Ranked by the evidence, in priority order: (1) BETTER CLASSICAL LINE-DETECTION ROBUSTNESS ranks first. Every deep-read source shows the same pattern: moving from ground-truth to predicted (automatic) line/segment detection costs 15-24 accuracy points at fixed solver, while the solver/optimizer itself is a secondary lever (TVCalib GT-seg AC@5 65-69% vs predicted-seg AC@5 42-58% [arxiv.org/abs/2207.11709]; PnLCalib's own refinement-module ablation shows the optimizer only adds +3.2 to +8.3 points FS [arxiv.org/abs/2404.08401]). This matches our own 2.6px classical-solve-when-lines-are-clean finding exactly — detection, not geometry, is the bottleneck. Concrete, precedented fixes exist: shadow removal preprocessing (MTMT+ShadowFormer) and ROI cropping to reject spurious lookalike lines [arxiv.org/pdf/2404.06977]. (2) MULTI-FRAME/STATIC-SPAN POOLING ranks second and is complementary, not competing, with (1) — it's the exact mechanism shown to turn per-frame catastrophic detection failure into graceful degradation (PMC6983053, PTZ-Calib). (3) MORE DIVERSE LABELS for a learned corner-finder ranks third: even TVCalib's segmentation net, trained on 14,513 broadcast images of one well-resourced sport, still drops 15-24 points going from GT to predicted detection — and no pickleball-specific dataset or pretrained model exists at all, so this route starts from zero transfer and we'd bear the full labeling cost with no guarantee it beats classical line detection. (4) A model-prior optimization route (replacing our classical solver with a learned one) ranks last — it targets the part of our pipeline (the solver) our own numbers show is already NOT the bottleneck.

### Q5
Q5 — No single open system solves our exact problem end-to-end (single static consumer camera, pickleball, ~5px, fully automatic) — confirmed honest null, nothing to adopt wholesale. But three components/patterns are worth borrowing rather than building from scratch: the gchlebus/tennis-court-detection classical Farin-method reimplementation as a reference baseline for line-model fitting (github.com/gchlebus/tennis-court-detection, no published accuracy numbers though — would need our own harness before trusting it); TVCalib's MIT-licensed reference implementation of the known-3D-model segment-reprojection-loss pattern (github.com/mm4spa/tvcalib); and PnLCalib's line-over-point refinement weighting result, which is directly actionable design guidance validating that we should keep prioritizing line evidence over corner/point evidence in our own solver (github.com/mguti97/PnLCalib). None of pb.vision, SwingVision, PlaySight, Zenniz, or Hawk-Eye publish open code or models for their calibration pipeline. What IS adoptable outright, cheaply, and proven-effective by their own product decisions is the CAPTURE-DISCIPLINE UX pattern itself (pb.vision's CourtFocus-style guided-framing lock-on before recording, mandatory static elevated mount, all-4-corners-visible enforcement) — this is a product/UX lever we can copy directly, independent of any CV work, and it is literally the entire explanation for pb.vision's <1px edge over us today.

## Ranked adoptions

This is product documentation, not a marketing performance claim. [help.pb.vision/articles/1108176-framing-and-court-alignment-guidelines]

## Q3. Is multi-frame/static-span pooling a documented technique — any precedent?

**Yes as a general principle in adjacent domains; no as a direct racket-sport precedent — and the two current sports-calibration SOTA papers explicitly say this is still open.**

- A 2020 study on multi-frame homography estimation for video stitching in static-camera settings pools feature-point evidence across a temporal window (a quantized 2D histogram of stable point locations, RANSAC sampling weighted by temporal-occurrence count) and measures **23–57% improvement in reprojection/stitching score** over per-frame estimation, with graceful degradation under noise where single-frame estimation fails catastrophically [MEASURED, pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6983053].
- **PTZ-Calib (2025)** is the closest production-grade analogue: it explicitly fuses evidence from multiple frames of the *same fixed-position camera* via feature tracking (union-find matching into "ray landmark" tracks across frames) plus incremental + periodic global bundle adjustment, rather than solving each frame independently. Measured results: mean IoU_part=96.8% (median 97.8%), focal-length error 1.52px mean / 0.97px median, rotation error 0.12°, translation error 0.08m; its incremental variant runs in 0.68s/image vs. 29.80s/image for the full offline bundle adjustment — i.e. "solve once carefully, reuse cheaply" is exactly the winning pattern in this closely analogous system [MEASURED, arxiv.org/html/2502.09075v1].
- **BHITK** (soccer field registration) models homography evolution frame-to-frame with a two-stage Bayesian filter (Linear Keypoint Filter + Extended Kalman Filter), so that when keypoints are sparse/occluded in a given frame the prior-frame forecast is retained via propagated covariance [arxiv.org/html/2311.10361v2].
- **Farin et al. 2004** (the foundational classical court-calibration paper still cited/reimplemented today) explicitly uses temporal tracking across frames to seed the next frame's optimization, precisely to stay robust when large parts of the court are occluded [dirk-farin.net/publications/data/Farin2004b.pdf].
- The amateur-tennis paper does a related but weaker version: it runs line detection across a series of frames and **picks the single best-scoring frame** by counting overlap between projected model lines and detected line pixels — closer to "select-best" than "average/median," but it is direct precedent for pooling across multiple frames of the same video before committing to one calibration [arxiv.org/pdf/2404.06977].
- Critically: **TVCalib (WACV'23) and PnLCalib (2026)** — the two most-cited current single-frame sports-calibration SOTA papers — both explicitly list "integration of temporal consistency" as **unaddressed future work** in their own text [arxiv.org/abs/2207.11709; arxiv.org/abs/2404.08401]. That means the specific pattern we're building tonight (pool line evidence across static frames → robust median → one authoritative lock → reuse for the clip → static-consistency guard with motion fallback) is not something the field has already built and shelved — it is a genuine, currently-open contribution, resting on solid precedent from adjacent tracking/stitching work but not yet done for sports-field calibration specifically.

**Honest null:** we found no racket-sport paper doing exactly this. Treat the design as sound-by-analogy, unproven-in-domain.

## Q4. Given the 2.6px classical solve already works when lines are detected, what closes the remaining gap fastest — diverse labels, better classical line detection, or model-prior optimization?

Ranked by the evidence, most to least promising:

**1. Better/more robust classical line-detection input quality (upstream of the solver).** Every deep-read source shows the identical pattern: the optimizer is not the bottleneck once given clean line evidence. TVCalib's own GT-vs-predicted-segmentation ablation shows a **15–24 percentage-point drop** in AC@5 purely from imperfect (automatic) line detection, with the solver held fixed [arxiv.org/abs/2207.11709]. PnLCalib's refinement-module ablation shows its optimizer stage adds only +3.2 to +8.3 points FS — a secondary lever compared to detection quality [arxiv.org/abs/2404.08401]. This matches our own 2.6px-when-clean finding exactly. Concrete precedented fixes: dedicated shadow-removal preprocessing (MTMT mask detection + ShadowFormer repaint) and YOLOv5-based court-ROI cropping to suppress spurious lookalike lines before fitting, both used in the amateur-tennis paper specifically to handle noisy consumer footage [arxiv.org/pdf/2404.06977].

**2. Multi-frame/static-span pooling.** Complementary to #1, not competing — it's the exact mechanism shown (PMC6983053, PTZ-Calib) to convert per-frame catastrophic detection failure into graceful degradation. Should compound with better line detection.

**3. More diverse labels for a learned corner-finder.** Ranks lowest on current evidence. Even TVCalib's segmentation net — trained on 14,513 broadcast images of one well-resourced sport — still loses 15–24 accuracy points moving from GT to its own predicted detections [arxiv.org/abs/2207.11709], consistent with our own PCK@5=0 result on owner viewpoints. No pickleball-specific dataset or pretrained model exists anywhere (confirmed gap, arxiv/GitHub sweep + ryan-tolone.com), so this route starts from zero transfer and requires us to fund 100% of the labeling cost with no guarantee of beating classical detection improvements.

**4. Model-prior optimization route (replacing the classical solver).** Ranks last — it targets the part of the pipeline our own numbers already show is *not* the bottleneck (2.6px demonstrated when lines are clean), and both TVCalib's and PnLCalib's own ablations confirm the optimizer/refinement stage is a secondary, not primary, lever.

**No complete system to adopt wholesale (honest null) — but three specific components/patterns are worth borrowing, plus one product-UX pattern worth copying directly.**

- **gchlebus/tennis-court-detection** — open classical reimplementation of Farin's Hough-line + geometric court-model fitting method [github.com/gchlebus/tennis-court-detection]. Worth using as a reference baseline for our line-detection stage, but it publishes **no quantitative accuracy numbers**, so it would need validation on our own harness before trusting it — an honest null on its actual precision.
- **TVCalib** (MIT-licensed code) [github.com/mm4spa/tvcalib] — reusable as a reference implementation of the "known-3D-model + differentiable segment-reprojection-loss" optimization pattern; soccer-specific and single-frame, so no direct accuracy transfer, but the design pattern (optimize camera params directly against detected line/point segments, no training data needed for the optimizer itself) is worth mirroring.
- **PnLCalib** [github.com/mguti97/PnLCalib] — its refinement-module ablation is directly actionable: line-based refinement drives the real accuracy gains, point-only refinement barely helps — confirms we should keep weighting line evidence over corner/point evidence in our own solver, which is already our direction.
- **Capture-discipline UX pattern** — pb.vision's CourtFocus guided-framing lock-on and mandatory static/elevated/all-corners-visible setup is copyable *as a product feature* right now, independent of any CV work, and it is the entire explanation for their <1px edge over our current harness [help.pb.vision/articles/1108176].
- No open code, model, or dataset from SwingVision, Hawk-Eye, Zenniz, or PlaySight was found — their advantages are either patented pipelines (SwingVision) or fixed multi-camera hardware installations (the rest), neither of which is adoptable for a single-consumer-phone product.

## What we should actually do — ranked

1. **Fix line-detection input quality first**, ahead of solver work or corner-finder retraining — this is the single most evidence-backed lever (15–24pt accuracy swings from detection quality alone across TVCalib and PnLCalib, matching our own 2.6px-when-clean/0-when-not split). Concretely: adapt the amateur-tennis paper's shadow-removal + ROI-cropping preprocessing recipe to our line detector [arxiv.org/pdf/2404.06977].
2. **Ship the static-span pooling + robust-median lock + static-consistency-guard build tonight as planned** — it's precedented in adjacent domains (video stitching: +23–57% reprojection gain; PTZ-Calib: incremental multi-frame fusion at 44x lower cost than full re-solve) and is explicitly ahead of what TVCalib/PnLCalib (current sports-calibration SOTA) have published. Use TVCalib's threshold-and-reject self-verification pattern as the shape for the fallback/guard logic.
3. **Weight line evidence over point/corner evidence** in the solver, per PnLCalib's own refinement ablation — reinforces current direction, don't second-guess it.
4. **Deprioritize a "more diverse labels" push for the learned corner-finder** relative to (1) and (2) — no pickleball pretraining exists anywhere to transfer from, and even soccer's best-resourced segmentation net still loses double-digit accuracy points from detection error alone. Revisit only after (1)/(2) are exhausted and still show a detection-quality gap.
5. **Copy pb.vision's capture-discipline UX pattern as a product feature** (guided framing / lock-on before recording, enforced static elevated mount) — cheap, proven by a real competitor's product decision, and directly closes the gap our own harness already measured as <1px.
6. **Do not expect to find or license an off-the-shelf system that already solves this** — confirmed honest null across academic literature, open-source repos, and production competitors. We are building at or near the frontier for this specific sport/precision/automation combination, not catching up to an existing solved product.
