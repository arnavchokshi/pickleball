## 2. pb.vision decoded (the 11-minute study)

Data banked at `data/pbvision_11min_20260713/` (cv_export 12.4MB, insights 41 rallies, stats, and
`source_video.mp4` 697.4s/720p/30fps, sha `272a2132...`). **This is pb.vision's OWN demo video
(uploader admin-ryan, "Demo Vid", 2024-12) - strictly R&D reference: never GT, never training data,
never redistributed.** Dual-model study (gpt-5.6-sol forensics 8/8 + a Fable 5-lens workflow with
adversarial re-computation) agreed on every number to the digit. Full record:
`runs/research_pbv11_20260713/` (SYNTHESIS.md, FORENSICS_11MIN.md, SOL_SURVEY.md, CROSSCHECK_RULING.md)
and the earlier single-rally study `runs/research_pbv_reveng_20260712/`.

**Their pipeline (decoded):**
1. Per-frame detector heads output ball position **plus dedicated shot/bounce/net event heads plus an
   apparent-radius head**. (We output ball position only - this is the whole gap.)
2. One **global ballistic track per rally** over all candidates; a single threshold on their track
   posterior reproduces **96.1%** of their frame selection. Membership uses low-confidence candidates
   via track consistency, not per-frame acceptance.
3. Emission window = **serve-contact -> terminal-event** with fixed pads (~0.9s pre-serve, ~1.1s tail);
   interior holes are tiny (23 spans, max 24 frames across 11 min). 75.6% in-rally coverage is
   *deliberate trimming*, not dropout.
4. Pure-gravity ballistics (drag not identifiable even on 130 long arcs); event delta-v is 39-44x interior.
5. In-sequence bounces pinned **exactly** to court-plane z=radius (154/154); nets pinned to net plane;
   out-of-range landings **clamped in-bounds with a `corrected` flag**.
6. **Rally-level fail-closed:** a rally with no viable track is dropped from the product entirely
   (cv rally 35 = the one zero-3D rally is absent from insights). They are fail-closed like us.
7. Radius depth cue is **universal-ordinal but confidence-gated** (R2 0.27 raw -> 0.71 at radius-conf>=0.7).

**Where we already win / are ahead:** our 2D detection coverage BEATS theirs (80.6% vs 58.7%); our
camera solve matches theirs to 2.7cm/0.27deg; and our honesty architecture is *ahead* - their in/out +
landing calls are binary with **zero uncertainty**, they ship a placeholder stat (`net_impact_score`
= 0.5 for everyone), and low-confidence shots still ship full facts. Their product fact *breadth* is
~10x ours (shot taxonomy, score state, win-prob strip, Wilson-CI coach advice) - that is an NS-05
build-out behind our honesty layer, not a CV gap.

### 3.2 The convergent diagnosis (what the wall actually is)
Every failure above points to the same place, now verified from multiple angles: **the ball is at its
worst exactly at the moment of contact** - fastest (motion-blur streak, not a dot), most occluded
(next to the player/paddle), and where our detector's position error is largest. Geometry-only
methods die there because (a) 2D candidates at contact are too noisy for a corner fit and (b) long
segments (~4.4s) contain multiple *unmarked* direction changes an arc can't legally bridge.

pb.vision sidesteps this with **trained event heads that fire on player appearance** (swing pose,
ball-near-paddle) - they don't need a clean ball at contact. **The gap reduces to: they have trained
contact/event detection; we have zero event labels.** Not solver math, not camera, not emission
policy, not candidate density.
