# Single-Camera 2D→3D Ball Lifting: What pb.vision Likely Does, and What This Project Should Adopt

## 1. Methods Taxonomy (trade-offs)

| # | Method family | Core mechanism | Anchor/segmentation model | Needs training / GT? | Strengths | Weaknesses (esp. for us) |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | **Segment-then-fit ballistic physics** (our current design; also MonoTrack, the Maiden-AI patent family, PB Vision's own job-posting description) | Detect 2D ball candidates → detect hard anchor events (bounce/contact) → per-segment nonlinear least-squares fit of a gravity+drag(+spin) ODE to the 2D reprojection | **Hard**, upstream, independent of the fit | No (classic optimization) | Interpretable, physically grounded, no training data, scipy-native, matches the openly-documented commercial pattern | Brittle: if anchor detection misses/mis-times an event, the whole segment either fits garbage or falls back — this is *exactly* tonight's failure (9/11 segments → `fit_bvp_fallback`, 96/248 inliers) |
| B | **Physics-search / joint anchor-fit** (TT3D) | Treat the bounce **state** (location+timing) itself as a free variable inside the same reprojection-minimization, found via ray-plane intersection with a known court/table plane, rather than trusting an independently detected anchor | Anchor **is** an optimization output, not an input gate | No | Reuses our exact scipy least_squares TRF+huber machinery; only changes what's held fixed vs. free; directly attacks anchor-starvation | Still needs *some* signal to seed segment boundaries at the whole-rally level (solved via DP, see C) |
| C | **Global whole-rally segmentation** (TT3D) | Run 2D detection over the *entire* rally first, then a dynamic-programming piecewise-polynomial fit finds all bounce/segment boundaries jointly and at sub-frame precision, before any 3D optimization | Global, DP-based, not local heuristics | No | More robust to per-window noise than our current audio-onset/wrist-velocity/inflection heuristics; quantifies confidence per segment | More implementation surface than A/B; still assumes the underlying 2D track has *enough* signal somewhere in the rally |
| D | **Lift-first, segment-second** (TT4D, arXiv:2605.01234) | Invert the pipeline: lift the *entire unsegmented* 2D track to 3D via a learned network first, then find bounces/hits as peaks/troughs in the now-unambiguous 3D signal | Segmentation happens **after** lifting, in 3D space | **Yes** — learned uplifting net trained on synthetic physics data (MuJoCo) | Explicitly built to survive exactly our failure mode (occlusion, gaps, weak/missing anchors); authors report lift-first beats segment-first (18.95cm vs 21.71cm mean 3D error) | Requires training a lifting network — a real net-new model, in tension with "scipy-first, no new training" house style; no in-domain 3D GT needed (trains on synthetic ballistic sims) but is a genuine architecture change |
| E | **Learned uplifting with real-artifact robustness** (Kienzle et al., WACV 2026, arXiv:2511.20250) | Two-stage: real-2D-supervised front-end perception + back-end uplifting transformer trained *exclusively* on synthetic physics data, using RoPE timestamps to natively tolerate missing detections/variable frame rate | Soft, built into the architecture | Yes, synthetic-only for the lifting stage | Directly targets "noisy, imperfect real detections" as the named motivating problem — i.e., the field's own SOTA line diagnosed our exact symptom and is fixing it architecturally | Same training-cost objection as D; validated on table tennis, not pickleball |
| F | **Soft-boundary learned canonical lift** ("Where Is The Ball", arXiv:2506.05763) | LSTM height-predictor + autoregressive gap-filling; end-of-trajectory probability is an **auxiliary** signal, never a hard cut; refinement network deliberately avoids forcing exact reprojection match to noisy 2D | Soft/probabilistic, not a gate | Yes, simulation-only training | Strong noise robustness (NRMSE 0.03→0.14 under ±25px noise); handles multi-bounce as one continuous sequence | Needs LSTM training; the *design lesson* (soft priors, not hard cuts) is more portable to scipy than the model itself |
| G | **End-to-end physics-informed detector** (PIT, arXiv:2604.16895) | Bakes ballistic dynamics into the detector itself as a differentiable-physics inductive bias, jointly estimating velocity and bounce events in one forward pass | Physics constraint is architectural, not post-hoc | Yes, and evaluated on generic/synthetic particles, not real sports video yet | Names our exact anti-pattern explicitly ("traditional approaches segment first, then fit independently") | Immature — no sport-specific or real-footage validation found; classic "synthetic-only ceiling" risk this team has hit before |
| H | **Filter-based real-time approximation** (UKF, RoboCup single-camera) | Unscented Kalman Filter as a cheaper real-time substitute for the batch nonlinear-least-squares "gold standard" | Recursive, naturally regularizes toward the physics prior under sparse updates | No | Validated as a low-accuracy-loss, cheap substitute; *seeded from an adjacent well-fit segment* it degrades gracefully instead of diverging | Not a full replacement for well-anchored segments — best used as a **graceful-degradation fallback path**, not the primary solver |
| I | **Multi-modal event anchoring** (Zenniz 30-mic triangulation; tennis audio+visual fusion studies) | Contact/bounce timing from audio (or audio+visual fusion), not vision alone; visual coarse-windowing *gates* audio search (not the reverse) improves F-score ~30pp for audio, ~10pp for video | External to the 3D solver — an anchor-quality lever | No | Even purpose-built commercial "Hawk-Eye-lite" systems don't solve contact timing from vision alone; our audio-onset+wrist-velocity design is directionally right but single-mic | We may have the ordering backwards (audio anchor independent vs. visual-gated-audio) |
| J | **Implicit cues as extra anchor signal** (BlurBall, arXiv:2509.18387) | Jointly predicts ball position **and** motion-blur direction/magnitude; blur sharpens exactly at velocity-discontinuity frames (hits/bounces), giving sub-frame event timestamps "for free" from the same heatmap | Cheap add-on signal, not a new pipeline | No (post-hoc read of existing detector) | Directly increases anchor quality/inlier count without touching the solver or violating the CoTracker3/SAM2-ball kill list | Unverified on our WASB-HRNet outputs specifically — needs a quick internal experiment |
| K | **Bounded-tolerance + user-correction UX** (SwingVision) | Ship known, quoted error bars (line calls 97% within 10cm @60fps; speed ±10%, ~20% slower than radar) and let users manually fix mistagged shots rather than hiding failure | Presentation-layer, not a solver | No | Validates the owner's "smooth presentation with visible uncertainty" instinct as the credible commercial norm | Doesn't fix the underlying anchor/solver problem, only its visibility |
| L | **Crisp-hide-uncertainty (anti-pattern)** (Hawk-Eye criticism) | Displays a single confident line without its own known ~2-4mm error margin | — | — | — | Explicitly criticized in sports-science literature as misleading; the proposed fix is literally our own trust-bands doctrine — strong argument to *keep/strengthen*, not abandon, trust bands |

## 2. pb.vision Dossier (only what sources actually support)

**What's confirmed, first-party:**
- PB Vision's own job posting for "Senior Data Engineer, Pickleball Analytics" describes the 2D→3D pipeline as: take imprecise frame-by-frame CV output, then "iterate on 3D equations of motion, incorporating factors like air resistance **and the Magnus effect**" and "address imprecise data challenges through estimation techniques." [CORROBORATED — remotepython.com/jobs/5f9a63de04594dc7b7fd7c6e62a77b58](https://www.remotepython.com/jobs/5f9a63de04594dc7b7fd7c6e62a77b58/) This is architecturally **identical in shape** to our own scipy ballistic-fit-over-noisy-detections design — but it explicitly includes Magnus-effect (spin) modeling, which is currently on our kill list.
- Recording requirements are a single stationary camera, ≥4–5ft height, all four court corners visible, 1080p/30fps minimum, no fiducial markers beyond corner visibility. [help.pb.vision/help/articles/5886489](https://help.pb.vision/help/articles/5886489-how-to-record-your-pickleball-game) — structurally identical to our own single-fixed-camera + 15-pt PnP court-corner calibration constraint. This is strong evidence the camera-setup/calibration stage is **not** where PB Vision's edge lives; the gap, if real, is downstream in the lifting/solver stage.
- Their help-center openly states **95%+ accuracy is for shot detection and scoring**, not raw 3D trajectory precision, and is conditioned on strict recording compliance — naming the same failure modes we're fighting: ball/court color contrast, shadow, partial shade, and camera-stability drift breaking court alignment. [CORROBORATED — help.pb.vision/articles/6518303](https://help.pb.vision/articles/6518303-pb-vision-stat-concerns) This matters: "pb.vision looks great on the owner's clips" may be evidence of good shot/rally-level aggregation and presentation polish more than superior raw per-frame 3D precision.
- Founder Mike Arney has stated publicly (podcast) that the CV stack is an **adaptation of tennis-style tracking**, not a novel 3D-reconstruction breakthrough, running at ~5 minutes of compute per 1 minute of video across ~100 cloud GPUs, explicitly: "we haven't done any sort of technical optimization." [CORROBORATED — elderresearch.com podcast](https://www.elderresearch.com/resource/podcasts/computer-vision-and-pickleball-game-changing-ai-insights-mining-your-own-business-podcast/) This tempers the assumption of a hidden elegant algorithm — the visible gap may be compute/data volume and UX polish, not a fundamentally different lifting method.
- PB Vision exposes a **Partner API with a low-level "cv" export tier** ("from which everything else is derived"), plus in-app "Download Raw Data" that any user (no partner approval) can pull as JSON for their own clips. [help.pb.vision/articles/2010377](https://help.pb.vision/articles/2010377-advanced-insights-with-data-exports) The referenced public schema pages (`pb-vision.github.io/schemas-cv`) render only a bare version string via automated fetch — no field-level docs extracted in this pass.
- No engineering blog, patent, or technical talk under **PB Vision / Pickleball Vision AI Inc.**'s own name discloses their court-calibration or lifting algorithm internals. All public content stops at camera-setup instructions and marketing-level "AI/ML/CV" framing. [pickleballunion.com](https://pickleballunion.com/pb-vision-pickleball-ai/), [pb.vision](https://pb.vision/)

**Important negative/caveat finding:** US Patent 11,615,540 B2 ("Methods and systems to track a moving sports object trajectory in 3D using a single camera") is assigned to **Maiden AI Inc.**, a Seattle **cricket**-analytics startup (Fulltrack AI), **not** PB Vision. [CORROBORATED — image-ppubs.uspto.gov/.../11615540](https://image-ppubs.uspto.gov/dirsearch-public/print/downloadPdf/11615540) This patent surfaces prominently in searches for "single-camera 3D ball trajectory patent" and is easy to mis-cite as PB Vision's method — it is not confirmed to be connected to PB Vision at all. It remains useful only as an **independent reference architecture** for the same problem class (CNN 2D detection → RANSAC inlier selection → camera-projection-matrix + physics-model nonlinear least-squares fit, gaps bridged by the fitted curve, not silent fallback) — structurally identical to our own arc-solver design, which validates the *shape* of our approach without telling us anything PB-Vision-specific.

**Bottom line on the dossier:** Every disclosed or inferable fact about PB Vision's method (physics-equations-of-motion fit over imprecise CV output, tennis-CV lineage, standard single-fixed-camera corner-visible calibration, heavy but not architecturally novel compute) is **consistent with the same method family we already use**. There is no evidence PB Vision has solved single-camera monocular 3D lifting with some fundamentally different geometry or model — the two candidate explanations for "their output looks better on the owner's clips" are (a) better anchor/inlier engineering + Magnus/spin modeling + far more inference compute per clip, and/or (b) better failure-hiding/aggregation at the presentation layer. Both point toward incremental engineering, not a moat.

## 3. Ranked ADOPT-LIST (fitted to our scipy/arc-chain stack)

Ranked by (impact on tonight's diagnosed defect) × (fit with house constraints: scipy-first, no multi-camera, no new-model training, kill-list respected).

**#1 — Make anchor time/location a free variable inside the fit, not a hard upstream gate (TT3D pattern)**
- *Mechanism:* Instead of requiring a detected bounce/contact anchor to exist before fitting, let the bounce **state** (via ray-plane intersection with our already-computed court/net planes) be searched jointly with velocity+spin in the same `scipy.optimize.least_squares` call — pick whichever hypothesis minimizes reprojection error.
- *Defect addressed:* Directly attacks the 9/11 `fit_bvp_fallback` rate — the failure is anchor-starvation (96/248 inliers), and this removes the hard dependency on a pre-existing anchor.
- *Effort:* Medium — reuses existing TRF+huber solver and existing court/net plane math; changes what's held fixed vs. searched.
- *Kill criteria:* If fallback rate on the same diagnosed clip doesn't drop meaningfully (say, from 9/11 to <5/11) after this change, the bottleneck is candidate-density (WASB gaps), not anchor-gating logic — escalate to #3/#6 instead.
- *Source:* [arxiv.org/pdf/2504.10035](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.10035), [cogsys-tuebingen.github.io/tt3d](https://cogsys-tuebingen.github.io/tt3d/)

**#2 — Pin BOTH ends of a segment to high-confidence events, not just the start (MonoTrack pattern)**
- *Mechanism:* Constrain the optimization with a known/high-confidence 3D position at both the segment's start *and* end (e.g., net-plane crossing, court-line proximity at low height, or the next segment's fitted-arc start) rather than letting the far end float freely.
- *Defect addressed:* The classic cause of local-minima/local-init sensitivity in monocular lift — likely a contributor to weak fits on the 2 segments that did fit and to divergence on the fallback ones.
- *Effort:* Low-medium — mostly a constraint/bounds change on the existing least_squares call.
- *Kill criteria:* If tightening the far-end constraint doesn't reduce residual/reprojection error on the 2 currently-successful segments, the technique adds no value here — deprioritize.
- *Source:* [arxiv.org/pdf/1111.1400](https://arxiv.org/pdf/1111.1400) (MonoTrack), corroborated independently by TT3D's bounce-anchoring.

**#3 — Global whole-rally DP segmentation before per-segment fitting (TT3D)**
- *Mechanism:* Run 2D detection over the entire rally first; fit a piecewise-polynomial dynamic-programming segmentation to find all bounce/breakpoints jointly at sub-frame precision, replacing (or supplementing) audio-onset/wrist-velocity/inflection heuristics.
- *Defect addressed:* Anchor-detection is the named root cause of tonight's fallback rate; a global, noise-robust segmentation is more resistant to local heuristic failure than per-window candidate scanning.
- *Effort:* Medium-high — new module, but scipy/numpy-implementable (DP over polynomial fit costs), no training required.
- *Kill criteria:* If the DP segmentation, run on the same diagnosed clip, doesn't recover more/better-timed breakpoints than the current heuristic anchors (measured by inlier count), it's not worth the added pipeline complexity.
- *Source:* [arxiv.org/html/2504.10035v2](https://arxiv.org/html/2504.10035v2)

**#4 — Decouple inlier-detection into its own explicit pass (Maiden AI patent pattern)**
- *Mechanism:* Run a standalone RANSAC-style inlier-classification process over the *raw* candidate 2D detection stream, independent of segment-boundary detection, before/alongside the physics fit — rather than relying on segmentation to double as inlier filtering and the huber loss to clean up the rest post-hoc.
- *Defect addressed:* Tonight's diagnosis conflates "segment boundary detection" and "which detections to trust" into one fragile stage; splitting them is a structural fix, not a parameter tweak.
- *Effort:* Medium.
- *Kill criteria:* If inlier count (currently 96/248) doesn't rise on the diagnosed clip after adding a dedicated inlier pass, the candidate stream itself is the bottleneck (upstream WASB quality), not the filtering logic.
- *Source:* [image-ppubs.uspto.gov/.../11615540](https://image-ppubs.uspto.gov/dirsearch-public/print/downloadPdf/11615540)

**#5 — UKF-seeded graceful degradation as the fallback path (replacing the current fail-open absurd-3D fallback)**
- *Mechanism:* When a segment has too few inliers for a real batch least-squares fit, seed a lightweight UKF (filterpy) from the adjacent well-fit segment's terminal state (position+velocity, known g/drag), and let it propagate under sparse measurement updates rather than re-solving from scratch on noise. Naturally regularizes toward plausibility instead of diverging to z=23.5m/underground/>20m/s jumps.
- *Defect addressed:* This is a **direct, narrow patch for tonight's specific symptom** (the fail-open renderer bug) — cheaper and faster to ship than #1-#4, and orthogonal to them (can ship alongside the render-fix already booked separately).
- *Effort:* Low-medium — filterpy is a light dependency; state-seeding logic is straightforward.
- *Kill criteria:* If UKF-propagated fallback segments still produce implausible positions (outside court+net bounding volume) more than a small fraction of the time, the seeding/adjacency assumption is wrong — fall back further to hard-clamping/trust-band-suppression only.
- *Source:* [researchgate.net/.../Real-Time_3D_Ball_Trajectory_...RoboCup](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/262271861_Real-Time_3D_Ball_Trajectory_Estimation_for_RoboCup_Middle_Size_League_Using_a_Single_Camera)

**#6 — Motion-blur read as a free sub-frame event-timing signal (BlurBall)**
- *Mechanism:* Extract blur direction/magnitude from the existing WASB-HRNet heatmap footprint shape (no new detector) to get sub-frame-precision contact/bounce timestamps, directly raising anchor quality/inlier count.
- *Defect addressed:* Anchor sparsity/imprecision — a cheap experiment before any bigger architecture change.
- *Effort:* Low — read-only post-hoc analysis of existing heatmap outputs; does not touch kill-listed CoTracker3/SAM2-ball.
- *Kill criteria:* If blur signal is too weak/noisy on our resolution/frame-rate (30-60fps phone video vs. their likely higher-fps broadcast source) to sharpen event timing, drop it — cheap to falsify.
- *Source:* [arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18387](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18387)

**#7 — Visual-gates-audio ordering swap for contact windows**
- *Mechanism:* Use the coarse wrist-velocity/visual hit-candidate window to **constrain** the audio-onset search (not the reverse, which appears to be our current design), matching the tennis audio+visual fusion literature's reported ~30pp F-score gain for audio and ~10pp for video.
- *Defect addressed:* Anchor quality/precision for contact windows specifically.
- *Effort:* Low — reordering an existing fusion step, not a new component.
- *Kill criteria:* If reordering doesn't change contact-window precision/recall on a held-out labeled sample, current ordering was already effectively equivalent — revert.
- *Source:* [researchgate.net/.../Detection_of_ball_hits...audio_and_visual](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/261119682_Detection_of_ball_hits_in_a_tennis_game_using_audio_and_visual_information)

**#8 — Revisit the spin/Magnus kill-list ruling (owner decision required, not unilateral)**
- *Mechanism:* PB Vision's own job posting explicitly names Magnus-effect modeling as a core responsibility of their 2D→3D pipeline — the strongest available evidence that spin curvature may be a real differentiator for trajectory smoothness/realism, not the dead end the kill list assumed ("spin-from-solver").
- *Defect addressed:* Not tonight's fallback bug directly, but plausibly the "why does pb.vision's *successfully-fit* trajectory look more natural/pro" gap once fallback is fixed.
- *Effort:* Medium — adding a Magnus term to the existing gravity+drag ODE is a small model change; the real cost is the killed-list precedent needing owner re-adjudication given new evidence, and observability (do we have enough spin signal from 2D alone to constrain it, or does it just add unconstrained free parameters that hurt fit stability?).
- *Kill criteria:* If adding a Magnus term measurably increases fit variance/local-minima incidence on the 2 already-successful segments without visibly improving curvature realism, revert — this is exactly the kind of extra free parameter that could worsen, not fix, the anchor-starvation problem if added carelessly.
- *Source:* [CORROBORATED — remotepython.com job posting](https://www.remotepython.com/jobs/5f9a63de04594dc7b7fd7c6e62a77b58/)

**Deliberately NOT adopted (out of scope for this pass, flagged for awareness only):**
- Learned lift-first architectures (TT4D, Kienzle WACV2026, "Where Is The Ball") — genuinely the most relevant *new* research beyond the named prior list (TrackNet/WASB/TTNet/TT3D/MonoTrack/RacketVision/BlurBall/TOTNet), and directly targets our exact symptom, but requires training a new model on synthetic data — in tension with scipy-first/no-new-training house style. Worth a future owner decision if #1-#7 don't close the gap, not tonight's fix. [arxiv.org/abs/2605.01234](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01234), [arxiv.org/abs/2511.20250](https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20250), [arxiv.org/html/2506.05763v1](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.05763v1)
- PIT (physics-informed end-to-end detector) — immature, no real-sport validation, real risk of the synthetic-only-ceiling trap this team has already hit once (court CALV1 wave). [arxiv.org/abs/2604.16895](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.16895)

## 4. Proposed Ours-vs-PB Vision Comparison Protocol (no 3D GT needed)

Because no 3D ground truth exists for the owner's clips and PB Vision publishes no benchmark, use a **three-pillar no-GT scorecard**, matching the closest existing precedent for exactly this situation (real deployment, single/near-monocular racket sport, no 3D GT): [arxiv.org/pdf/2511.20250](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2511.20250)

1. **Reprojection consistency.** For every fitted segment (ours) and, separately, for PB Vision's exported ball track (if obtainable), reproject the 3D trajectory through the calibrated camera (our 15-pt PnP) and measure per-frame residual against the WASB 2D detections. Cheap, requires zero pb.vision cooperation for *our* half. [arxiv.org/html/2506.05763v1](https://arxiv.org/html/2506.05763v1)
2. **Court-anchored bounce pseudo-GT.** The bounce frame's 2D pixel, back-projected onto the known, fixed court plane via PnP, is a usable pseudo-ground-truth landing point independent of either solver — as long as the bounce frame itself can be identified in 2D (audio onset / trajectory inflection, which we already compute). Compare **both** our bounce point and PB Vision's bounce point against this same pseudo-GT on identical clips. [arxiv.org/pdf/2506.05763](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2506.05763)
3. **Physics-sanity check.** Re-integrate our solved arc under the same gravity+drag(+optional Magnus) model from its initial condition and measure divergence from the raw solved trajectory — flags segments the solver itself doesn't trust, orthogonal to reprojection error. [arxiv.org/html/2502.04483v1](https://arxiv.org/html/2502.04483v1) (methodology borrowed from 3D-human-pose physical-plausibility scoring, same no-GT logic)

**Statistical framing:** Since neither system is a gold standard, report **Bland-Altman bias + 95% limits of agreement** (not a single "accuracy" number) for any quantity both systems output on identical clips — bounce (x,y), apex height, speed-at-contact. This only shows how differently the two systems err; pillar #2 above is what breaks that symmetry for the landing-point axis specifically. [ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10358465](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC10358465/)

**Data acquisition path (owner action required):** PB Vision's in-app "Download Raw Data" (three-dot menu) exposes a low-level "cv" JSON export tier for any user's own clip — no Partner API approval needed. [help.pb.vision/articles/2010377](https://help.pb.vision/articles/2010377-advanced-insights-with-data-exports) This is the only way to get PB Vision's actual per-frame numbers for the comparison; automated WebFetch cannot log into the authenticated pb.vision viewer, so this cannot be done by a research pass alone — **route as an owner-action ask**: pull the "cv" export for the same reference clip we're diagnosing.

## 5. Open Questions

1. **Is PB Vision's visible edge really in raw 3D solve quality, or in aggregation/UX polish?** Their own help center states 95%+ is for shot detection/scoring, not trajectory precision — this needs the comparison protocol above to resolve, not assumption.
2. **Does reviving Magnus/spin modeling help or hurt fit stability?** PB Vision's job posting is first-party evidence it's part of their pipeline, but adding a free parameter to an already anchor-starved solver could make fallback worse before it makes trajectories more realistic — needs a controlled experiment on the 2 currently-successful segments before touching the kill list.
3. **Is our segment-then-fit architecture (Method A) fundamentally the wrong shape, or just under-engineered?** The 2025-2026 research lineage (TT4D, Kienzle, "Where Is The Ball") is converging on lift-first / soft-boundary designs as strictly better than hard segment-then-fit — but all require training a new model. If the #1-#7 adopt-list items don't close the fallback gap after implementation, this is the next owner decision: stay scipy-first with diminishing returns, or approve a first learned-lifting model trained purely on synthetic physics data (no in-domain 3D GT required by any of these papers).
4. **How much of PB Vision's advantage (if any, once measured) is just compute volume?** Founder's own account (~100 GPUs, 5x realtime, "no technical optimization") suggests brute multi-hypothesis 2D detection/smoothing compute rather than an exotic algorithm — worth checking whether ensembling/multi-pass WASB inference (cheap on our GPU fleet) closes some of the gap before any architecture change.
5. **Patent-attribution hygiene:** US 11,615,540 B2 (Maiden AI / cricket) must **not** be cited as evidence about PB Vision's specific method in any downstream doc — it's a same-shape reference architecture only, independently validating our approach, not describing PB Vision's.
6. **Can we get PB Vision's actual per-frame numbers at all?** Depends entirely on the owner pulling a "cv" export via in-app download — until that happens, all "pb.vision looks great" evidence remains visual/qualitative, not measured.
