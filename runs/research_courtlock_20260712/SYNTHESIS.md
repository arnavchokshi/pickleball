# Court-lock SOTA fan-out — 2026-07-12 (58 sonnet agents, 2-vote adversarial refutation)

Status: research evidence only. VERIFIED=0. [CORROBORATED] = survived 2-vote refutation; never a product gate pass.

## Key facts

- ITF's Hawk-Eye lab-test ground truth (Dec 2004) used a Phantom high-speed camera at 2000Hz/512x512px over a calibrated 0.5x0.5m FOV (~1mm/px), with ITF's own reference reading uncertainty of ±0.9mm — a physics-metrology rig, not an ML-labeled dataset (itftennis.com/media/12242/line-calling.pdf).
- Hawk-Eye Officiating (ITF-certified) is an 8-10 fixed-camera, 50/60Hz rig, each camera on its own PC, all on a wired isolated network feeding one control PC that fuses multi-view images against 'a virtual model of the court' — confirmed multi-view, not monocular (itftennis.com/media/6216, PAT-13-002 report, tested 6 Dec 2013).
- Hawk-Eye Officiating was ITF-approved 14 Oct 2005 and first used at Hopman Cup Jan 2006; Hawk-Eye Live (real-time) was not certified until June 2020 — a 14-year gap between offline-replay and live-call approval.
- As of Dec 2023 only 5 systems had passed ITF's line-calling evaluation: Hawk-Eye/Hawk-Eye Live, FOXTENN/FOXTENN Live, IMG Arena(FlightScope)/IMG Arena Live, and Bolt6 Sentinel; none of these regulatory documents disclose any algorithm, camera-calibration method, or license.
- SwingVision's own patent (US11893808B2, filed Nov 2020, Mangolytics Inc) describes a single 2D phone camera feeding a neural net trained to correlate object pixels (ball/player) with 'regulation topology' (known court-line geometry) to regress 3D properties end-to-end, not via an explicit homography/keypoint-solver pipeline — trained against ground truth from co-located radar/Lidar rigs at multiple physically instrumented events, not from labeled frames alone; no accuracy numbers, no architecture, no homography method disclosed.
- pb.vision (our reference diagnostic) requires a single STATIC camera (tripod/fence-mount, ≥5ft height, no handheld, no camera cuts) with all 4 court corners visible for the whole clip, gated by a pre-recording 'CourtFocus' lock-on UI, and explicitly rejects tournament/multi-angle footage — their production design pushes viewpoint-robustness burden onto capture UX/constrained input rather than solving arbitrary-camera-motion court tracking in the model (help.pb.vision).
- FOXTENN's in/out method is architecturally different from Hawk-Eye: ground-level synchronized high-speed cameras + laser scanners imaging the ball's actual court contact mark directly at up to 2500fps, rather than reconstructing/estimating trajectory from elevated multi-view cameras.
- Zenniz fuses vision with acoustics: patented 'Dual Tracking' = 4 cameras + a 30-microphone sound-triangulation array analyzing bounce impact acoustic signatures.
- TVCalib (WACV 2023, MIT license, github.com/mm4spa/tvcalib) reframes field registration as full 3D camera-parameter optimization via a differentiable segment-reprojection loss over line/point-cloud correspondences — the calibration/optimization step itself needs ZERO training data (only the upstream segmentation net does); homography-decompose-then-refine (structurally like our no-op step) is measurably worse than direct reprojection-loss optimization in their own ablation (AC@5 48.1% vs 68.7% GT-segments, SN-Calib-test-center).
- TVCalib's own best numbers cap out at AC@5px ≈65-69% even with ground-truth segment pixels (54.8-57.6% with predicted segments) — a PCK@5px≥0.95 gate is not achieved by SOTA soccer-field calibration even in-domain with GT input; pickleball's simpler rectangle-only geometry may behave differently but this is unproven.
- 'No Bells, Just Whistles' (CVPR-W 2024, arXiv 2404.08401v2) shows plain classical DLT+RANSAC calibration (NO learned refinement network) beats prior refinement-network SOTA on SoccerNet/WC14/TS-WorldCup, when fed a richer geometry-derived keypoint set (extra keypoints synthesized via line-intersection/conic constructions from the base annotated set) — but code is GPL-2.0 (copyleft, commercial-incompatible) and the upstream keypoint detectors still need 22,816+ labeled images to train.
- PnLCalib (arXiv 2404.08401, CVIU 2026, paper CC BY 4.0 but code GPL-2.0) adds a nonlinear least-squares refinement using BOTH detected lines and points (cost = 0.6*line_term + 0.4*point_term) on top of any existing keypoint/line detector output, improving JaC@5px by ~5-8 points across all tested soccer datasets with no detector retraining required — directly answers 'how to fix a no-op refinement step' with a concrete, license-clean-to-reimplement objective.
- MAGSAC++ (CVPR 2020) is a training-free, drop-in robust estimator (cv2.USAC_MAGSAC) that beats vanilla RANSAC on extreme-view-change homography fitting and is far less sensitive to its inlier threshold; Graph-Cut RANSAC reports ~10-15% accuracy gain, LO-RANSAC ~20% gain over vanilla RANSAC — all usable immediately with zero new labels.
- Plumb-line/Hough-entropy self-calibration (arXiv 0810.4426) recovers lens radial distortion purely from straight lines already in the scene (i.e., our own court lines) by minimizing Hough-space entropy after correction — a classical, zero-label method directly applicable to our 'wide-lens distortion is first-class' constraint.
- yastrebksv/TennisCourtDetector (MIT license) is trained on 8,841 images across hard/clay/grass courts using a <7px correctness threshold — the closest racket-sport precedent for sizing our own label budget and PCK gate, but it required thousands of labeled images across surface/venue variety, not ~100.
- A 2024 paper on amateur/messy tennis-court footage (arXiv 2404.06977) documents that classical Hough+homography court detection 'struggled to generalize efficiently' on dirty/amateur courts and fixed this by layering ML shadow-removal and object-detection on top of (not replacing) the classical geometric pipeline — a close structural analog to our own classical-Hough + learned-refinement setup and its generalization failure.
- None of the production line-calling vendors (Hawk-Eye, FOXTENN, IMG Arena, Bolt6) disclose their court-tracking/calibration algorithms in any of the sourced regulatory documents — these are certification/history records, not engineering specs, and give a target accuracy bar (mm-level, fixed multi-camera rigs) but no implementable method for a single moving phone camera.

## Systems intel

### Hawk-Eye Officiating / Hawk-Eye Live (ITF-certified)
- 8-10 fixed cameras around the court, 50/60Hz, each on a dedicated PC, wired isolated network, one control PC fuses multi-view images vs a 'virtual model of the court' to reconstruct 3D ball/player trajectories.
- ITF-approved 14 Oct 2005 (offline/review use); Hawk-Eye Live (real-time) not certified until June 2020 — 14-year gap.
- 2004 lab ground-truth used a separate Phantom high-speed camera (2000Hz, 512x512px, 0.5x0.5m FOV, ~1mm/px) with ±0.9mm reference uncertainty — this is the metrology rig, not the production camera array, and is not disclosed to be the production accuracy figure in these two ITF docs.
- No algorithm, calibration method, triangulation math, accuracy number, or license is disclosed in either ITF source read (line-calling.pdf or the PAT-13-002 approval report) — both are regulatory/procedural, not engineering documents.
Citations: https://www.itftennis.com/media/12242/line-calling.pdf ; https://www.itftennis.com/media/6216/pat-13-002-pat-approval-report-hawk-eye-rev-6.pdf

### SwingVision (Mangolytics Inc / Swingvision Inc)
- Patent US11893808B2 (filed Nov 2020, granted Feb 2024): single 2D phone camera; neural net correlates object-of-interest pixels with 'regulation topology' (known court-line geometry) pixels to infer 3D properties end-to-end, not via an explicit homography/keypoint-solver pipeline.
- Training ground truth comes from co-located radar/Lidar sensors at multiple physically instrumented 'training events' over 'many hours of play' — a capital-intensive sensor-fusion data-acquisition regime we cannot replicate.
- No accuracy numbers, network architecture, dataset size, or license/code are disclosed anywhere in the patent; it is a legal filing, not an engineering spec.
- Sued by Infinity Cube Ltd. (maker of ITF-approved 'eyes3' hardware line-call system) for patent infringement, April 2022, S.D. California — establishes SwingVision as a software/phone-camera competitor operating in contested patent space, not itself ITF-certified for line calls.
Citations: https://patents.google.com/patent/US11893808B2/en ; https://news.bloomberglaw.com/ip-law/patent-suit-targets-ex-tesla-engineers-tennis-ball-tracking-app

### pb.vision (our reference diagnostic)
- Requires a single STATIC camera (tripod/fence/stable mount, ≥5ft height), all 4 court corners visible for the entire clip, landscape only, 1080p30 minimum (4K/60 gated behind paid tier), H.264/.mp4, HDR and image stabilization explicitly disabled.
- Explicitly rejects multi-angle/tournament-cut footage: 'our AI requires that the same shot be seen throughout the entire video' — confirms no per-cut/per-segment re-calibration exists in their pipeline; one fixed extrinsic calibration is assumed for the whole video.
- Ships 'CourtFocus,' an in-app real-time lock-on UI that guides the user to center the court BEFORE recording begins — alignment is gated at capture time, not solved purely by post-hoc arbitrary-viewpoint calibration.
- No architecture, training-data regime, or license/accuracy numbers are disclosed on this help-center page; server-side processing takes ~30 minutes per clip (not real-time).
Citations: https://help.pb.vision/articles/1108176-framing-and-court-alignment-guidelines

### FOXTENN Top Real Precision System / FOXTENN Live
- Ground-level synchronized high-speed cameras + laser scanners at line ends imaging up to 2500 frames/second, directly capturing the ball's actual contact mark on the court surface at bounce — a non-inferential, non-trajectory-estimation approach fundamentally different from Hawk-Eye.
- ITF-approved Dec 2016 (review mode); FOXTENN Live approved Nov 2021; first tournament use 2017 ATP Moselle Open.
Citations: http://foxtenn.com/post/why-an-electronic-line-calling-on-clay ; https://www.itftennis.com/media/12242/line-calling.pdf

### Zenniz Dual Tracking
- Fuses two sensing modalities: 4 high-resolution cameras plus a patented 30-microphone acoustic-triangulation array analyzing ball-impact sound signatures for bounce localization, rather than relying on vision alone.
Citations: https://zenniz.com/smart-corner/how-does-zenniz-work

### PlaySight SmartCourt
- Permanent multi-fixed-camera installation (full-court + net + surrounding coverage), ~$20,000/court install cost plus monthly fee, oriented mainly at stroke/tactic/player-statistics analytics rather than solely automated line-calling — a fundamentally different permanently-installed-multicam product model vs a single moving phone.
Citations: https://www.researchgate.net/publication/397366512_Automated_Tennis_Player_and_Ball_Tracking_with_Court_Keypoints_Detection_Hawk_Eye_System

### ITF phased certification protocol (structural pattern, not a vision method)
- Nov 2003 ITF/WTA/ATP protocol defines 3 phases: (1) lab accuracy/usability test under controlled conditions, (2) 'shadow' test at a live tournament (calls recorded, not used by officials), (3) 'live' test where officials actually act on the system's calls.
- Multiple independent vendor architectures (Hawk-Eye, FOXTENN, IMG Arena/FlightScope, Bolt6) all passed the same protocol, implying the certification bar is architecture-agnostic even though the document discloses no per-vendor method — only the phased validation structure itself, not any vision technique, is transferable to us.
Citations: https://www.itftennis.com/media/12242/line-calling.pdf

## Now vs after labels

Implementable with ZERO new labels (classical/geometric or training-free, usable immediately on the existing stack): replacing the broken homography-refinement step with a TVCalib-style differentiable segment-reprojection-loss pose optimizer (MIT, no training data needed) or a PnLCalib-style points+lines nonlinear-least-squares refinement (reimplement the objective — code is GPL-2.0 so don't vendor it, but the CC-BY-4.0 paper's formula is free to reimplement); swapping vanilla RANSAC for MAGSAC++ (training-free, one-line OpenCV change) or reimplementing GC-RANSAC/LO-RANSAC patterns; adding multi-initialization+argmin robustness and a reprojection-loss-threshold self-verification/abstention gate (both zero-label patterns from TVCalib, and the latter directly serves the product's honest-uncertainty requirement); synthesizing extra "virtual" geometric keypoints from our existing corner taps via line-intersection constructions (the "No Bells" trick, reimplemented to avoid its GPL license); plumb-line/Hough-entropy self-calibration of lens radial distortion straight from our own court lines (no calibration target needed); adopting ELSED/DeepLSD as a faster or more precise off-the-shelf line-segment detector feeding these stages; copying pb.vision's CourtFocus capture-time alignment-gating UX pattern; and structuring an internal ITF-style lab->shadow->live phased validation gate for any of the above. What needs the ~100 owner labels (or more): fine-tuning a small racket-sport-precedented keypoint CNN (yastrebksv-style, MIT-licensed, <7px threshold) as a true held-out cross-venue eval to test whether 100 labels move our measured 290px cross-source-transfer number at all — this is the most direct test of the labeling bet, but the corpus gives no racket-sport evidence that 100 labels (versus thousands, as every SOTA soccer/tennis keypoint detector used) is sufficient; and any move toward denser grid-keypoint architectures (KpSFR) or novel constraint-network architectures (CourtKeyNet) that were only validated in the source corpus on thousand-image-scale datasets, whose code licenses are additionally unconfirmed. What needs gold/instrumented capture beyond even 100 labels: SwingVision's own disclosed method (radar/Lidar-instrumented training events) and Hawk-Eye/FOXTENN/Zenniz's fixed multi-camera or multi-sensor (audio+vision) rigs are both structurally inapplicable to a single moving phone and would require capital/hardware investments (dedicated sensor rigs, fixed installations) outside the labeling-budget question entirely — none of these production competitors offer a monocular-moving-camera method we can adopt at any label count, which is itself the clearest finding: no primary source in this corpus discloses a working single-moving-camera production court-tracking algorithm; every disclosed production system is either fixed-multi-camera (Hawk-Eye, FOXTENN, PlaySight, Zenniz) or a black-box patent with no method detail (SwingVision), or explicitly a single-STATIC-camera product (pb.vision) — so closing our per-frame temporal tracking gap for a moving camera has no direct production precedent to copy at all, labels or not.

- Is 100 owner-labeled diverse-venue frames enough to meaningfully move our measured 290px cross-source keypoint-transfer failure, given every SOTA racket-sport/soccer keypoint detector in this corpus (yastrebksv: 8,841 images; SoccerNet-Calibration: 22,816 images; TS-WorldCup: 3,812 images) was trained/fine-tuned on far larger label counts than we plan to collect?
- What is Hawk-Eye's actual disclosed production (not lab-rig) accuracy figure and underlying algorithm — neither ITF document read discloses camera-count-specific triangulation, calibration, or Kalman-filtering method, or a single authoritative current production accuracy number, so any 'Hawk-Eye = 2.6mm' claim circulating elsewhere remains unconfirmed by primary sources fetched here.
