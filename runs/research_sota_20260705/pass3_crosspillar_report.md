# PASS 3 — Final gap + newest-2026 sweep across ball prediction/physics, human ID/mesh, paddle 6DoF, and end-to-end production integration

2026-07-06. 29 agents.

## Scope and method

This sweep covers material from roughly the last 6-9 months (through June 2026) plus a few load-bearing corrections to previously-catalogued items, focused on (a) genuinely new work across ball physics/tracking, human ID/mesh, paddle 6-DoF, and coaching, and (b) how real single-camera sports-3D systems achieve production reliability. All numbers below are as stated in primary sources; claims that survived adversarial verification are marked **[VERIFIED]**; claims where the verification pass found a correction are marked **[CORRECTED]** with the fix inline.

## 1. Ball physics, tracking, and failure recovery

**TT4D** (arXiv 2605.01234, May 2026) is the single closest published analog to a joint ball+player+camera reconstruction pipeline for a racket sport, and it targets table tennis, not pickleball. It inverts the conventional segment-then-reconstruct order: it lifts an *entire unsegmented* 2D ball track to 3D via a transformer trained on 3M synthetic MuJoCo trajectories, using a Disentangled Context Embedding and Deferred Upsampling Token Attention to gracefully handle missing detections, then performs temporal segmentation and spin estimation in the 3D domain. Reported errors: 3D trajectory error 2.35±1.03 cm under clean conditions, degrading to 3.78±2.15 cm under combined missing-detection + half-FPS stress; full-point (whole-rally) processing beats per-segment processing by ~12% (18.95 cm vs 21.71 cm mean error) — https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01234. The lifting network runs at >500 points/sec on a "10-year-old Titan X." The dataset (146 hours / 211,534 curated points from 45,946 broadcast games, 29.6% yield after filtering) is stated for CC BY 4.0 release, but **no code repository was confirmed** — treat as a paper/dataset-only reference for reimplementation, not a drop-in system.

**[VERIFIED]** TT4D's most novel finding for our paddle-6DoF problem: racket orientation and velocity at impact are recovered *not* by visually tracking the racket but by inverting the observed post-impact 3D ball trajectory through an optimal-control two-point boundary-value problem (RK4 integration + Magnus-effect physics), reaching 26.4±4.4° orientation error and 0.58±0.40 m/s velocity error against 92 motion-capture-validated strokes (avg impact speed 3.72 m/s) — https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01234. Adversarial verification confirmed this is stated near-verbatim in the paper, including the authors' explicit motivation that "direct monocular tracking of the racket is often unreliable due to its high velocity, small size, and frequent occlusions" — directly matching our own racket-6DoF ablation finding that boxes dominate and fingers are rest-pose-weak.

**RacketVision** (arXiv 2511.17045, AAAI 2026 Oral, MIT-licensed code at github.com/OrcustD/RacketVision, 72 stars) remains the only large-scale benchmark unifying ball tracking, articulated racket pose (5 keypoints/racket, 2D only), and trajectory forecasting across table tennis/tennis/badminton (1,672 clips, 435,179 frames, 942 broadcasts). Its most actionable finding: naive concatenation of racket-pose features into ball-trajectory prediction *hurts* accuracy versus a ball-only baseline (e.g., table tennis long-horizon ADE 139.9 vs 113.9), while Cross-Attention fusion (ball trajectory as Query, racket poses as Key/Value) beats ball-only (101.3 ADE) — a validated architectural lesson for any ball+paddle joint model we build.

**TrackNetV5** (arXiv 2512.02789, Dec 2025) adds Motion Direction Decoupling (signed polarity fields fixing V4's directional-ambiguity problem) and a Transformer-based Residual-Driven Spatio-Temporal Refinement head, reporting F1 0.9859 / recall 0.9797 vs V4's F1 0.9581 / recall 0.9225 on the TrackNetV2 tennis benchmark, at only +3.7% FLOPs. **This is directly relevant since we just vendored TrackNetV4** — but the "official SDK" (github.com/codelancera-offical/TrackNetV5-SDK) is fully proprietary, "all rights reserved," restricted to "technical exchange and academic study only," with no released weights or training data — a hard licensing blocker even though our constraints allow internal-only use, since there is no grant at all for modification/redistribution.

**[VERIFIED]** The sequential-hypothesis-testing failure-detection framework (arXiv 2602.12983, WACV 2026 workshop, CC BY 4.0) reframes tracker monitoring as an anytime-valid e-process test via Ville's inequality: P(sup_t X_t ≥ 1/α) ≤ α, giving a provable false-alarm bound with no "peeking penalty," needing no retraining and negligible compute. Empirically: FPR 0.00-8.20% and detection delay 22-178 frames across OTB-100/LaSOT/TrackingNet/GOT-10k with KCF and SiamFC trackers. One caveat found in the qualitative results: unsupervised metrics (peak correlation) can miss a failure entirely if the tracker locks confidently onto the wrong object.

A hybrid physics+NN trajectory model (arXiv 2503.18584, 2025) needs only ~90 real trajectories per ball type (closed-form drag/Magnus axis equations + a small MLP predicting just 3 coefficients), runs at 0.97 ms average inference, and includes a pickleball test split (90 train/103 test, 300-1000 ms flights) — though pickleball results are qualitative-only ("satisfactory," no numeric error given).

**[CORRECTED]** The "Pickleball Flight Dynamics" paper (arXiv 2409.19000) is commonly assumed to give measured aerodynamic coefficients, but it is a pure simulation study: its drag coefficient Cd≈0.6 is borrowed by analogy from wiffle-ball literature (±0.5 stated uncertainty), not measured on real pickleballs, and spin/Magnus lift is explicitly excluded ("we ignore the impact of spin"). It should not be used as a calibrated physics prior without independent validation.

The Event-based Gaze Control paper (arXiv 2606.26780, Jun 2026) treats every ball contact as the start of a new free-flight trajectory and explicitly re-initializes tracking at each contact event for real-time spin estimation — a useful re-initialization pattern, though it requires an event camera, which is outside our single-consumer-iPhone constraint.

## 2. Human ID/mesh: unified scene+human reconstruction

Several 2026 papers extend the Human3R feedforward family (which we've already catalogued) toward joint scene+human reconstruction, all built on the **VGGT** backbone (1.2B-param transformer, feedforward camera+depth+point-map prediction, arXiv 2604.06830):

- **UniCon3R** (arXiv 2604.19923, Apr 2026): adds a scene-aware contact prompt fed back into mesh regression, cutting Human3R's floating-artifact distance from 33.56 cm to 5.50 cm and penetration depth from 6.40 cm to 1.59 cm on RICH, and improving WA-MPJPE from 109.9 mm to 81.5 mm. Runs at 2.40 FPS on a single A5000. **Code/checkpoints not yet released** ("will be released soon").
- **SHOW / "Scene and Human in One World"** (arXiv 2606.27720, Jun 2026): injects human-scale priors into point-map prediction to fix monocular scale ambiguity, reaching 3DPW PA-MPJPE 41.0 mm vs Human3R's 44.1 mm and >20x better human-scene alignment (HS-V5 0.030 vs 0.652). Trained only on synthetic BEDLAM 2.0 (4,250 sequences); authors explicitly admit a sim-to-real generalization gap. No confirmed code release.
- **UniSH** (arXiv 2601.01222, Jan 2026) is a contemporaneous, closely related unified feedforward method.
- **Multi-HMR 2** (arXiv 2606.14841, Jun 2026, NAVER Labs): distills SAM2 memory features during training so the model gets appearance-based multi-person identity tracking at inference *without ever seeing video/track supervision*. This is potentially relevant to our 4-player identity-persistence problem, but **no quantitative metrics, code repo, or license could be independently confirmed** (PDF exceeded fetch size limits) — treat as unverified pending full access.
- **OnlineHMR** (arXiv 2603.17355, Mar 2026) targets streaming/online rather than offline-batch world-grounded mesh recovery, relevant to real-time constraints.

**[VERIFIED, partially corrected]** Depth Anything 3 (arXiv 2511.10647, Nov 2025, code+weights public at github.com/ByteDance-Seed/Depth-Anything-3, mixed Apache-2.0/CC-BY-NC-4.0 licensing across variants) is cited across these papers as a VGGT successor. Adversarial review found the headline superiority numbers are **internally inconsistent across the authors' own official channels** (project page/tech report say 35.7% pose / 23.6% geometry improvement over VGGT; the arXiv abstract metadata says 44.3%/25.1%), and independent third-party testing (GitHub issue #29) found DA3 only marginally better than VGGT on ScanNet++ and worse on some datasets, with strong results partly dependent on the authors' own re-annotated ground truth. DA3 is real, usable today (code+weights live), but its magnitude of advantage over VGGT should not be taken at face value.

## 3. Paddle/racket 6-DoF: no dedicated pickleball method exists

The clearest gap confirmed by this sweep: **no 2026 paper targets pickleball-paddle or racket 6-DoF pose estimation as a dedicated method** (as opposed to a dataset). The closest generic hand-held-object methods are:

- **ForeHOI** (arXiv 2602.06226, code+weights at github.com/Tao-11-chen/ForeHOI, trained on synthetic GraspXL renders): a feedforward model reconstructing 3D geometry + 6-DoF pose of an arbitrary held object from monocular video in under 1 minute, needing only first-frame point clicks and no CAD model — directly generalizes our paddle-in-hand problem, but is not racket-specific and real-world generalization from purely-synthetic training is unverified.
- **RGBTrack** (arXiv 2506.17119, Jun 2025): depth-free 6D tracking by feeding zeroed "fake depth" into a depth-based tracker, maintaining tracking through occlusion/rapid motion using RGB features + previous pose.
- **DynamicPose** (arXiv 2508.11950, IROS 2025) and **Event6D** (arXiv 2603.28045, CVPR 2026): retraining-free 6D tracking via VIO-guided Kalman filtering and event-camera-based tracking respectively — both address fast/occluded object tracking but neither is racket-specific, and Event6D requires an event camera.

**[VERIFIED]** synthesized finding: neither TT4D (ball+body+spin+segmentation, but racket only inferred indirectly, no court reconstruction) nor RacketVision (ball+racket pose, but 2D-only, no body mesh, no court) jointly covers all four components (ball, full body mesh, paddle 6-DoF, court) for any racket sport in a released system — confirming this remains an open integration problem industry-wide, not just for pickleball.

## 4. Multi-player identity and court-state reconstruction

**SoccerNet-GSR** (arXiv 2404.11335/2504.06357, code at github.com/SoccerNet/sn-gamestate, GPL-3.0 code / CC BY 4.0 dataset) is the most directly analogous released reference architecture for single-camera-to-structured-world-state reconstruction: a modular calibration→detection→ReID→tracking→jersey-OCR pipeline with a purpose-built **GS-HOTA** metric (LocSim × IdSim, 5m Gaussian tolerance, requiring exact role+team+jersey-number match) for scoring structured reconstruction rather than raw tracking. **TrackID3x3** (MMSports'25, arXiv 2503.18282, github.com/open-starlab/TrackID3x3, Apache-2.0 core / MIT BoT-SORT / CC-BY-NC-3.0 jersey-OCR submodule) demonstrates the identical pattern at small-fixed-roster (6-player) scale for 3x3 basketball. No pickleball-specific 4-player identity/mesh paper was found; production tools (DinkVision, PB Vision, Ryan Tolone's system) publicly describe only shot/ball analytics, not identity-persistence architecture.

## 5. Coaching and production-reliability lessons

**"Synthesizing the Expert"** (arXiv 2605.12799, May 2026, CC BY-NC-SA 4.0) is the most directly relevant primary source on AI-coaching trustworthiness: a 4-stage LLM pipeline auto-accepted 97.4% of 1,914 synthetic swimming-coaching Q&A triplets after rule-based critique, recovered 79.1% of initial rejects via corrective regeneration, caught 27 hallucination instances via a single rejection rule (no unsupported reference to values/drills/protocols absent from retrieved context), and explicitly gated drill prescriptions below 0.6 stroke-detection confidence to prevent conflating fatigue artifacts with technique flaws. Authors admit a "Synthetic-to-Real Gap" requiring live validation before operational deployment.

Other coaching-adjacent 2026 work: **SportsGPT** (arXiv 2512.14121, Dec 2025) uses DTW-based motion alignment + a knowledge-based diagnostic model + LLM feedback generation, beating general LLMs on diagnostic accuracy in ablations (no head-to-head vs Talking-Tennis/CoachMe found). **COACH** (arXiv 2512.01853, Dec 2025) proposes a reconfigurable multi-agent system with a Critic agent enforcing step-by-step verification for sports video understanding. **AgentCoach** (CHI 2026) is an LLM adaptive-coaching system whose Phase 1 evaluation explicitly includes pickleball forehand drive as a test skill.

On raw production-pipeline economics: "Optimizing Video Analytics Inference Pipelines" (arXiv 2512.07009) found postprocessing dominates naive pipeline latency (40.2 ms of 55.8 ms/frame total), reducible to 11.4 ms via GPU-accelerated postprocessing — a concrete profiling lesson.

**[CORRECTED]** Two USPTO patents (US11045705B2, US12478848B2) are sometimes attributed to "PB Vision" but are assigned to **Nex Team Inc** (HomeCourt basketball app, NBA-partnered), verified directly via Google Patents assignee records. The disclosed method — ground-projection-line hypothesis + parabolic curve fit + back-projection-error scoring on a mobile device, explicitly ignoring air resistance/spin — is a real shipped monocular-mobile 3D-trajectory system, but is basketball-specific and its physics assumptions (no drag/Magnus modeling) are a poor fit for pickleball's spin-heavy dinks.

## Where our constraints bite

Every unified human+scene method with the strongest reported numbers (UniCon3R, SHOW, UniSH) has **no confirmed public code**, forcing a choice between waiting, reimplementing from papers, or using the one backbone that is actually shippable today (VGGT or DA3, both public) despite DA3's contested advantage margin. Similarly, TrackNetV5's clear accuracy gain is blocked by a fully proprietary SDK license — even though our internal-only usage would otherwise be acceptable, there is no license grant at all. TT4D's dataset-only release means its most valuable idea for us (trajectory-inversion for paddle 6-DoF) requires full reimplementation, not adoption. Scarce in-domain pickleball data remains the deepest structural gap: no source in this sweep provides validated pickleball aerodynamic coefficients, no pickleball-specific paddle-pose or 4-player-identity paper exists, and the hybrid physics+NN model's ~90-100-trajectory data requirement is the only concretely low-cost path found to close that specific gap ourselves.


## Ranked options

### Adopt TT4D's 'lift-first' architecture pattern + ball-trajectory-inversion for paddle impact state
- **what:** Reimplement the core architectural ideas from TT4D (arXiv 2605.01234): lift the entire unsegmented 2D ball track to 3D before temporal segmentation (vs segment-then-reconstruct), use Disentangled Context Embedding + Deferred Upsampling Token Attention to handle missing detections gracefully, and infer paddle orientation/velocity at impact by inverting the observed post-impact 3D ball trajectory via an optimal-control two-point boundary value problem rather than visually tracking the small fast paddle.
- **evidence:** [VERIFIED] 3D ball trajectory error 2.35-3.78cm depending on stress conditions; racket orientation error 26.4±4.4° and velocity error 0.58±0.40m/s vs 92 mocap strokes; full-point processing beats per-segment processing by ~12% (18.95cm vs 21.71cm mean error) — https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01234
- **expected_gain:** Directly addresses our stated pain points: held-out shot misses under occlusion, and the 'fingers rest-pose-weak, boxes dominate' paddle-tracking ablation finding from our own racket-6DoF lane — this offers a physics-grounded alternative path to paddle 6-DoF that sidesteps visual paddle tracking weaknesses entirely
- **cost:** High — no code release confirmed, requires reimplementing the lifting network, DCE/DUTA masking, and OCP-based racket inversion from the paper description alone; needs synthetic MuJoCo-style pickleball trajectory generation for training data (analogous to their 3M synthetic points)
- **risk:** Table tennis paddle-ball impact dynamics (rigid racket, short rally segments) may not transfer cleanly to pickleball paddle (different mass/restitution, longer flight times, more spin variety); two-point BVP inversion assumes short flight + moderate spin for a unique solution — pickleball's longer dink flights may violate this assumption
- **confidence:** med

### Wrap existing ball tracker in sequential-hypothesis-testing failure detection
- **what:** Define a bounded per-frame quality metric from our ball tracker's existing containment/anchor-recovery signals and wrap it in the e-process sequential test (arXiv 2602.12983) to get a statistically-guaranteed, anytime-valid failure alert with bounded false-alarm rate, replacing any ad hoc confidence thresholds.
- **evidence:** [VERIFIED] P(sup_t X_t ≥ 1/α) ≤ α via Ville's inequality; empirical FPR 0.00-8.20% and detection delay 22-178 frames across OTB-100/LaSOT/TrackingNet/GOT-10k with two trackers; needs no retraining and adds negligible compute — https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.12983
- **expected_gain:** Cheap, principled upgrade to our ball-tracking QA layer that could reduce both false failure-alerts and missed genuine track failures with a provable statistical guarantee, directly useful for the production-reliability gap this sweep targeted
- **cost:** Low — no retraining, just needs our own bounded quality metric definition (e.g. adapt our containment score to [0,1]) and the aGRAPA/SF-OGD betting-rate update logic, which is described in enough algorithmic detail in the paper to reimplement without code
- **risk:** Paper's own qualitative failure case shows unsupervised metrics (peak correlation) can miss failures entirely when a tracker confidently locks onto the wrong object — our ball tracker's quality metric would need to avoid this failure mode (e.g. prefer a metric closer to their supervised NGIoU than pure internal confidence)
- **confidence:** high

### Evaluate Depth Anything 3 as a scene-geometry/camera backbone
- **what:** Prototype swapping our current camera-pose/scene-geometry front end for DA3 (code+weights already public, some variants Apache 2.0), given it's cited as beating VGGT (the common backbone across UniCon3R/SHOW/UniSH) and its streaming variant runs under 12GB GPU memory for long single-take video.
- **evidence:** Partially verified — DA3's own official sources give inconsistent superiority numbers over VGGT (35.7%/23.6% vs 44.3%/25.1%), and independent third-party testing found DA3 only marginally better or worse than VGGT on some datasets (ScanNet++, TartanAirV2-WB), with some strong results dependent on the authors' own re-annotated ground truth — https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.10647, https://github.com/ByteDance-Seed/Depth-Anything-3
- **expected_gain:** If real, a straightforward drop-in geometry-backbone upgrade with confirmed public code/weights (unlike the still-unreleased UniCon3R/SHOW/UniSH) that could improve camera-space localization accuracy across our whole pipeline
- **cost:** Medium — code/weights are available today so integration cost is mainly evaluation/benchmarking on our own iPhone footage, not reimplementation
- **risk:** The magnitude of DA3's advantage over VGGT is unverified/inconsistent per adversarial review; real-world gain on our specific single-consumer-camera pickleball footage is unknown and could be marginal
- **confidence:** med

### Adopt UniCon3R-style contact-aware corrective refinement for foot/paddle grounding
- **what:** Borrow UniCon3R's core mechanism — feeding a contact estimate back into mesh regression as an internal corrective prior before final SMPL-X output — to address our own documented foot/root floor-sink and jitter issues (VP-C floor-sink root cause from our visual-polish lane).
- **evidence:** UniCon3R (arXiv 2604.19923) reduced RICH-dataset float distance from 33.56cm to 5.50cm and penetration depth from 6.40cm to 1.59cm vs Human3R baseline, with contact-guided latent refinement being the single largest ablation contributor to WA-MPJPE improvement (90.2mm to 81.5mm)
- **expected_gain:** Directly targets a known, already-diagnosed weakness in our own pipeline (VP-C floor-sink); could meaningfully reduce foot jitter beyond our existing smoothing-based VP-C2 fix
- **cost:** High — no code/checkpoints released yet ('will be released soon' per repo as of fetch); would require full reimplementation of the scene-aware contact prompt and Visual Prompt Tuning adaptation of frozen Human3R/CUT3R backbones
- **risk:** Runs at only 2.40 FPS on an A5000 — not real-time; unclear how well the contact-prior mechanism transfers to a court/floor scene (flat, textureless) vs the richer indoor scenes UniCon3R was evaluated on (RICH, EMDB, SLOPER4D)
- **confidence:** low

### Build a lightweight hybrid physics+NN pickleball trajectory model from ~100 own-captured trajectories
- **what:** Replicate the architecture from arXiv 2503.18584 — closed-form drag/Magnus-informed axis equations with a small MLP predicting only the 3 hardest-to-fit coefficients, trained on ~90-100 real captured trajectories per condition — using our own iPhone-captured pickleball flight data instead of relying on the unvalidated simulation-only Cd≈0.6 prior from the pickleball flight dynamics paper.
- **evidence:** Hybrid model needs only ~90 real trajectories/ball-type, runs at 0.97ms average inference (MLP component 0.018ms), and was already tested on a pickleball split (90 train/103 test, 300-1000ms flights) with 'satisfactory' (unquantified) accuracy — https://arxiv.org/html/2503.18584v1. By contrast, the only pickleball-aerodynamics paper found (arXiv 2409.19000) is a pure simulation with an analogy-borrowed Cd (±0.5 uncertainty) and explicitly excludes spin/Magnus effects, so it cannot be used as a calibrated ground-truth physics prior as-is.
- **expected_gain:** Realistic, low-data-cost path to a real-time, sport-specific trajectory forecaster/physics-consistency check, feasible given our single-iPhone capture setup and the paper's demonstrated low sample requirement
- **cost:** Medium — requires collecting and hand-labeling ~100 real pickleball trajectories (position/spin) via our existing capture pipeline, plus reimplementing the closed-form equation fitting + small MLP (code link provided is data-only, not confirmed to include training/inference code)
- **risk:** Paper gives no quantified pickleball error numbers (only qualitative 'satisfactory'); model doesn't handle bounce dynamics (pre/post-bounce segments only); pickleball's real drag/lift coefficients remain unmeasured by any source found, so the physics-equation family's constants would need independent calibration
- **confidence:** med

### Reference SoccerNet-GSR / TrackID3x3-style modular pipeline for multi-player identity + court reconstruction
- **what:** Mirror the released, modular calibration→detection→ReID→tracking→attribute-recognition architecture (and GS-HOTA-style LocSim×IdSim evaluation metric) from SoccerNet-GSR and TrackID3x3 for our up-to-4-player pickleball identity/court-state problem.
- **evidence:** SoccerNet-GSR baseline is fully released (GPL-3.0 code, CC BY 4.0 dataset) with swappable calibration/detection/ReID/tracking/OCR components — https://github.com/SoccerNet/sn-gamestate; TrackID3x3 (Apache-2.0 core, MIT BoT-SORT submodule, CC BY-NC 3.0 jersey-OCR submodule) demonstrates the same pattern at small-fixed-roster (6-player) scale — https://github.com/open-starlab/TrackID3x3
- **expected_gain:** A proven, working reference architecture for structured multi-entity world-state reconstruction from a single camera that we can mirror component-for-component, including an identity-aware evaluation metric design we currently lack
- **cost:** Medium — components are for different sports (soccer/basketball) and would need retraining/adaptation for pickleball's 2-4 player fixed roster and much smaller court
- **risk:** Jersey-number-OCR-style identity resolution may be irrelevant/inapplicable in pickleball (no jersey numbers typically); the GPL-3.0 license on SoccerNet-GSR code specifically may conflict with internal-only licensing preferences depending on how it's incorporated (though stated constraint says internal-only is acceptable)
- **confidence:** med

### Adopt 'Synthesizing the Expert' validation blueprint for AI coaching feedback trust
- **what:** Apply the pattern of retrieval-grounded generation + explicit domain rejection rules + confidence-gated escalation to human review + logged hallucination detection (as used in arXiv 2605.12799 for swimming coaching) to our own future pickleball coaching-feedback generation layer.
- **evidence:** 4-stage LLM pipeline auto-accepted 97.4% of 1,914 synthetic Q&A triplets after rule-based critique, recovered 79.1% of rejects via regeneration, caught 27 hallucination instances via a single rejection rule, and explicitly gates drill prescriptions below 0.6 stroke-detection confidence — https://arxiv.org/html/2605.12799v1
- **expected_gain:** A concrete, evidence-based blueprint for building a trustworthy AI-coaching validation layer before we build any user-facing feedback generation, addressing a currently unresearched part of our roadmap
- **cost:** Low-medium — this is a process/architecture pattern, not code to integrate; requires designing our own domain-specific rejection rules and confidence thresholds for pickleball coaching claims
- **risk:** Authors themselves flag a 'Synthetic-to-Real Gap' — their corpus is population-level, not individual-athlete-specific, and needs live validation before operational deployment; direct transferability from swimming physiology domain rules to pickleball biomechanics is unverified
- **confidence:** med

- TrackID3x3 (MMSports'25, arXiv 2503.18282, code at github.com/open-starlab/TrackID3x3, Apache-2.0 for original code, MIT for BoT-SORT submodule) is a released, working small-fixed-roster (6 players) multi-object tracking + jersey-OCR + court-relative identity pipeline for 3x3 basketball — the jersey-number-pipeline submodule specifically is CC BY-NC 3.0, restricting commercial reuse of that piece only
- SoccerNet Game State Reconstruction baseline (github.com/SoccerNet/sn-gamestate, GPL-3.0 code, dataset CC BY 4.0) is a released, modular single-broadcast-camera pipeline (calibration→detection→ReID→tracking→jersey-OCR) with a purpose-built GS-HOTA metric (LocSim x IdSim, 5m Gaussian tolerance) for scoring structured world-state reconstruction rather than raw tracking accuracy — https://github.com/SoccerNet/sn-gamestate
- No published, code-released system was found that jointly reconstructs ball + full player body mesh + paddle/racket 6-DoF + court in one pipeline for pickleball or any closely analogous racket sport; TT4D is closest for ball+body+spin+segmentation but infers racket only indirectly and doesn't reconstruct court geometry, while RacketVision does ball+racket pose but only in 2D with no body mesh or court — synthesized from https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.01234 and https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.17045
- ForeHOI (arXiv 2602.06226, Feb/Jun 2026, code+weights at github.com/Tao-11-chen/ForeHOI, trained on synthetic GraspXL renders) is a feedforward (non-optimization) model reconstructing 3D geometry + 6-DoF pose of an arbitrary held object from monocular hand-object video in under 1 minute, needing only first-frame point clicks (no CAD model) — a generic analog to our paddle-in-hand problem, though not racket-specific and real-world generalization from synthetic training is unverified
- 'Synthesizing the Expert' (arXiv 2605.12799, May 2026, CC BY-NC-SA 4.0) is the most directly relevant AI-coaching trustworthiness blueprint found: a 4-stage LLM pipeline with 12 domain rejection rules auto-accepted 97.4% of 1,914 synthetic swimming Q&A triplets, recovered 79.1% of rejects via corrective regeneration, and explicitly gated any drill prescription below 0.6 stroke-detection confidence to prevent hallucination — a transferable pattern (ground claims in retrieved context + confidence-gated human escalation) for our own coaching-feedback validation layer
- 'Optimizing Video Analytics Inference Pipelines' (arXiv 2512.07009, 2025/2026) found postprocessing dominates a naive pipeline's latency (40.2ms of 55.8ms/frame total), reduced to 11.4ms via GPU-accelerated postprocessing — a concrete production-latency lesson for our own pipeline profiling
- Two USPTO patents (US11045705B2, US12478848B2) belong to Nex Team Inc (HomeCourt basketball app), NOT PB Vision as commonly assumed — corrected via direct Google Patents assignee check; the disclosed method (ground-projection-line hypothesis + parabolic curve fit + back-projection-error scoring, ignoring air resistance/spin) is a real shipped monocular-mobile 3D trajectory system but is basketball-specific and explicitly ignores drag/Magnus effects relevant to pickleball spin
- No pickleball-specific 4-player identity/mesh-tracking research paper was found in the 2026 sweep; production tools (DinkVision, PB Vision, Ryan Tolone's system) use YOLOv8 detection + pose estimation but publicly describe only shot/ball analytics, not identity persistence architecture — https://ryan-tolone.com/projects/pickleball/
- Industry commentary (2026) frames pickleball as lacking affordable AI-driven shot-tracking/line-calling/feedback solutions despite >150% recent participation growth, unlike tennis — a market-context data point, not a technical one — https://digitaldefynd.com/IQ/ai-use-in-pickleball/
