# Ball Detection, Tracking, and Monocular 3D Trajectory Reconstruction — Racket Sports Landscape Review (2023–2026)

## 1. The core landscape fact: no pickleball-specific SOTA exists

Across every search conducted (arXiv, GitHub, product/press sources) covering 2023–2026, **no pickleball-specific ball-detection or 3D-reconstruction academic paper or dataset was found**. All current SOTA racket-sport ball-tracking/3D work — the TrackNet family, WASB-SBDT, BlurBall, TOTNet, RacketVision, TT3D/TT4D, MonoTrack, "Where Is The Ball" — targets tennis, badminton, table tennis, soccer, volleyball, or basketball only [https://arxiv.org/html/2511.17045v3]. This confirms the brief's stated binding constraint: **zero in-domain pickleball training data exists in the published literature**, and any progress must come from either (a) transferring/adapting cross-sport SOTA, (b) synthetic data, or (c) the owner's own upcoming captures.

## 2. 2D ball detection SOTA (candidate detector-zoo additions)

**WASB-SBDT** (BMVC 2023, arXiv:2311.05237, MIT license) is our current best zero-shot detector. Its HRNet-style high-resolution modules + position-aware training + center-of-heatmap inference report F1=94.0/Acc=89.0/AP=91.0 at τ=4px on its own in-domain **Tennis** dataset (TrackNetV2), beating the prior best method (MonoTrack) by 7.8–16.8 AP points across 5 sports, at only 1.5M parameters vs. 11.3M (TrackNetV2) [https://github.com/nttcom/WASB-SBDT]. Note this in-domain F1@4px=94.0 metric is **not directly comparable** to our own held-out F1@20px=0.7248 zero-shot pickleball result — different dataset, different pixel threshold, different domain. The repo has had zero commits since 2023-11-23 (unmaintained) [same source].

**TrackNetV4** (ICASSP 2025, arXiv:2409.14543, MIT license) adds a lightweight "motion prompt layer" (frame-difference attention) that can be dropped into any TrackNetV2/V3-style heatmap regressor. Gains are small but consistent: F1 86.53→88.13 and recall 82.32%→84.13% on the paper's new tennis dataset, at only ~3–4% speed cost (241→232 FPS) [https://github.com/TrackNetV4/TrackNetV4]. A further evolution, **TrackNetV5** ("residual-driven spatio-temporal refinement," arXiv:2512.02789), shows the lineage is still active into late 2025 [https://arxiv.org/html/2512.02789], though its numbers were not independently reviewed in depth here.

**BlurBall** (arXiv:2509.18387, Sept 2025) jointly predicts ball position and motion-blur/velocity cues in one head, explicitly motivated by fast racket-sport balls appearing in few frames [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2509.18387] — directly relevant to pickleball's fast, small, often-blurred ball. It is already used as an external ball detector inside TT3D's pipeline [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.10035].

**TOTNet** (arXiv:2508.09650, Aug 2025, MIT license, github.com/AugustRushG/TOTNet) is the strongest occlusion-specific result found. It retains the temporal axis via 3D convolutions (rather than channel-stacking), and adds a 4-level visibility-weighted BCE loss plus occlusion augmentation. On fully-occluded tennis frames, it cuts RMSE from WASB's 264.45 to 15.31 (with optical flow) and raises fully-occluded accuracy 0.33→0.67 (no-OF) or up to a claimed 0.80 on its own new TTA dataset (vs WASB's 0.63) [https://arxiv.org/abs/2508.09650]. Its own ablation shows occlusion augmentation ALONE actually *worsens* results (RMSE 29.57→54.26) — the weighted loss and augmentation must be combined [same source]. It is explicitly framed as an offline method (12–28 FPS), matching our cloud-processing (non-real-time) model.

**RacketVision** (arXiv:2511.17045, AAAI 2026 oral, MIT license, github.com/OrcustD/RacketVision) is the first large unified multi-sport benchmark (1,672 clips/435,179 frames, table tennis + tennis + badminton — no pickleball or padel) [https://arxiv.org/html/2511.17045v3]. Its best model, "MS-TrackNetV3," reaches precision/recall/mAP of 0.945/0.880/81.9 (tennis) and 0.915/0.865/83.1 (badminton), vs. weaker 0.924/0.762/71.1 (table tennis, the hardest domain in this benchmark) [same source]. Two findings are directly actionable: background modeling alone cuts mean distance error by 54–61% across sports, and **multi-sport joint training** improves ball-tracking mAP by +19.2% (tennis) and +14.6% (badminton) vs. single-sport training [same source] — relevant if we ever add non-pickleball racket-sport data as auxiliary training signal. Naive concatenation of racket-pose features *hurts* trajectory prediction, while Cross-Attention fusion (ball as Query, racket as Key/Value) helps — a concrete recipe if we pursue joint ball+paddle modeling [same source].

## 3. Monocular 3D trajectory lifting (closest analogues to our stated #1 gap: no true 3D mid-air ball position)

**"Where Is The Ball"** (arXiv:2506.05763, June 2025, CC BY 4.0) is the closest published match to our stated gap. It represents each 2D detection as a camera-independent "canonical ray" (intersection with ground plane + a vertical reference plane), removing camera-parameter dependence, and trains an LSTM pipeline (End-of-Trajectory net + Height net + Refinement net) **purely on synthetic Unity/PhysX data — zero real 3D/multi-view supervision** [https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.05763]. Zero-shot on real tennis (TrackNet, 81 broadcast clips), it achieves 87.21% landing accuracy / 0.63m error vs. a SynthNet baseline's 53.35% / 3.58m [same source]. However, real-world sim-to-real gaps are sport-dependent and sometimes large: real Mocap error 3.83cm/2.15cm (vs. synthetic 1.33cm/0.48cm) but real IPL-soccer error 119.15cm/26.04cm — nearly two orders of magnitude worse than its own synthetic soccer numbers [same source]. **Code and the promised real 3D dataset were still marked "coming soon" as of the fetched project page** — not yet usable off-the-shelf.

**MonoTrack** (arXiv:2204.01899, 2022) is the earliest full end-to-end monocular-3D system in this class (badminton), combining court/pose detection with a physics (drag-ODE) optimizer fit against 2D reprojection + hit/receive endpoint constraints — structurally the closest existing analog to our own IRLS ballistic-arc-between-anchors solver [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2204.01899]. It reports strong hit-segmentation (F1 0.946) but only 8.1–37.1px 3D reconstruction accuracy end-to-end, and ~20% of shots fail 3D reconstruction outright due to hit-type misclassification cascading into the physics solver [same source] — a cautionary data point: physics-based bounce/hit segmentation errors propagate hard into 3D accuracy, closely mirroring our own "1 teleport" and bounce-heuristic weaknesses.

**TT3D** (arXiv:2504.10035, code released MIT/CC BY-SA 4.0, github.com/cogsys-tuebingen/tt3d) validates our specific architectural choice: segmenting trajectories into free-flight arcs via bounce/hit detection, then fitting per-segment physics ODEs (drag + Magnus + gravity) by minimizing 2D reprojection error [https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.10035]. On 130 synthetic trajectories with realistic noise, side-view reconstruction succeeds 97.3% of the time at 12.4cm MAE, but degrades notably for less favorable camera angles: back-view MAE is 29.8cm, more than 2× worse [same source] — directly relevant to our single, sometimes-handheld consumer camera setup, where viewing angle is not always favorable.

**TT4D** (arXiv:2605.01234, 2026) is the most advanced published table-tennis system and the most directly relevant to our *joint ball+paddle* ambitions. Rather than segment-then-lift, it uses a "lift-first" transformer trained exclusively on 3 million MuJoCo-synthetic trajectory points, achieving 2.35±1.03cm 3D position error and 16.72±11.88 Hz spin error synthetically, and — critically — recovers **racket orientation and velocity at impact purely from the ball's own observed trajectory via an inverse-dynamics (two-point boundary value) optimization, with no racket detector or tracker in the loop**: 26.4±4.4° orientation error, 0.58±0.40 m/s velocity error across 92 real recorded strokes [https://arxiv.org/pdf/2605.01234]. It runs at >500 reconstructed points/second even on a decade-old Titan X GPU, well within our A100 40GB budget [same source]. Its dataset release is promised (CC BY 4.0) but no code repo URL was found for the lifting network itself.

**Uplifting Table Tennis** (arXiv:2511.20250, WACV 2026, GPL-3.0, code released) offers a template two-stage pipeline (front-end 2D detector cross-validated against a second detector via DBSCAN clustering + back-end transformer trained on 140,000 synthetic MuJoCo trajectories with a custom time-aware RoPE) that is robust to degraded input: under combined half-FPS + 10% missing detections stress, it retains 97.1% spin accuracy and 5.75px error vs. a prior baseline's 67.7%/23.54px [https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.20250] — directly relevant to our own noisy, gap-prone detection stream.

## 4. Competitor and industry landscape: is single-camera 3D actually solved elsewhere?

This is where evidence most cuts against an optimistic read. **PB Vision**, our named competitor, discloses **zero technical detail** publicly — the site is a client-rendered SPA with no architecture, training data, or accuracy figures anywhere found, and its capture protocol (stationary tripod, ≥4ft high, all four court corners + net tape visible, 1280×720, async upload-then-process) is the only verifiable fact [https://help.pb.vision/articles/5886489-how-to-record-your-pickleball-game]. A US patent (11,615,540B2) initially thought to be pb.vision's architecture was, on adversarial verification, found to belong to an **unrelated cricket-analytics company (Maiden AI Inc./"Fulltrack AI")** — its LM+RANSAC/per-axis physics-model description [https://patents.google.com/patent/US11615540B2/en] should be treated as an analogous method, not pb.vision's actual approach.

More tellingly, **PlayReplay** — the AI vendor selected by Pickleball Inc. (the sport's governing/commercial body) for automated line-calling debuting on PPA Tour/MLP in 2026 — uses a **four-camera** system mounted on net posts, not one, cross-confirmed by two independent primary press releases [https://pickleball.com/news/pickleball-inc-partners-with-playreplay-to-bring-automated-line-calls-and-ai-tracking-to-the-sport-of-pickleball, https://www.playreplay.io/news/pickleball-inc-partners-with-playreplay]. Its 100M-call/250K-match track record and ITF Silver line-calling classification are stated for its **tennis** deployments; no pickleball-specific accuracy numbers exist yet. This aligns with the broader pattern: **Hawk-Eye** uses 6+ triangulating cameras [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawk-Eye], and **Zenniz** explicitly fuses 4 cameras with 30 audio sensors "because video alone was judged insufficiently accurate/robust for line-calling" [https://zenniz.com/smart-corner/how-does-zenniz-work]. The sport's own most scrutinized, best-funded live commercial deployment as of 2026 has not chosen monocular capture for high-stakes accuracy — this should temper (not eliminate) confidence that single-camera 3D "very accurate" tracking is a solved, production-proven capability elsewhere, even though our own product doesn't require line-calling-grade precision.

## 5. Existing pickleball-specific fine-tuning attempts (small but real external data points)

Two small, non-peer-reviewed pickleball fine-tuning attempts exist and are directly comparable to our own repeated failures. A University of Rochester senior-design project fine-tuned TrackNetV2 (badminton-pretrained) on 12,000 manually labeled frames from a single Sept 2021 match: the badminton zero-shot baseline scored ~53% TPR on pickleball, rising to only **~65% TPR** after fine-tuning the final 14 conv layers over 20 epochs, with the team explicitly noting **no cross-match validation was performed** [https://www.hajim.rochester.edu/senior-design-day/pickleball-analytics/]. A secondary blog write-up of what appears to be the same project lineage claims much higher numbers (94.07% accuracy/93.47% TPR/87.81% precision) [https://www.hudsong.dev/pickleball], but adversarial verification found this is likely a **later, more overfit run** on the same single-match data, with the author's own text explicitly flagging overfitting and poor generalization to angled/occluded views [same source, verified]. **[VERIFIED with correction]**: treat the 53%→65% TPR figure as the more credible, methodologically-disclosed result — it is directionally consistent with (not contradicting) our own 4 measured internal fine-tune failures.

Separately, Roboflow's May 2026 blog fine-tuned RF-DETR-Small (Apache 2.0, github.com/roboflow/rf-detr) on just 118 total labeled images across 4 classes (Player/person/Paddle/Pickleball), reporting 76.6% mAP@50/84.5% precision/77.3% recall/80.7% F1 [https://blog.roboflow.com/automate-pickleball-analytics/] — **[VERIFIED]** these are explicitly aggregate metrics across all 4 classes, not ball-isolated, so they cannot be directly compared to our ball-only F1 metric, and the tiny dataset likely contains far fewer than 118 ball instances.

## 6. Data-efficiency techniques for our scarce-data constraint

Given the brief's flagged binding constraint (zero in-domain data, owner captures starting soon), two techniques stand out as not-yet-tried and well-evidenced elsewhere. **Semi-supervised teacher-student pseudo-labeling** (Vandeghen et al., CVPR 2022 workshop, arXiv:2204.06859, BSD-3, github.com/rvandeghen/SST) shows a confidence-weighted "doubt zone" pseudo-labeling scheme improving soccer ball/player mAP from 18.1% to 26.2% at just 1% labeled data (193 frames) plus ~55k unlabeled frames, with gains at every labeled-data scale tested and further compounding over a second training iteration (39.5%→43.7%→45.1% at 10% labeled) [https://arxiv.org/abs/2204.06859]. This recipe maps directly onto our situation: our existing WASB+BlurBall+TrackNet ensemble could serve as an initial "teacher" over the owner's large volume of upcoming unlabeled game footage.

**Meta's SAM3/3.1** (blog March 2026, code github.com/facebookresearch/sam3, custom "SAM License," 848M params) offers concept/text-prompted segmentation that could in principle speed up manual labeling of new captures — but adversarial verification found CVAT's own SAM3 integration (changelog March 26, 2026) still does **not** support video-level mask propagation across a rally; only per-frame prompting is shipped today [https://www.cvat.ai/resources/changelog/sam3-text-prompts], meaning the "prompt once, propagate across a rally" labeling-acceleration story is not yet turnkey.

## Ranked options (synthesis agent; manager re-derives decisions)

### 1. Adopt TOTNet-style occlusion-aware architecture/training recipe (visibility-weighted loss + occlusion augmentation + optional 3D-conv temporal encoder) on top of our existing WASB/BlurBall/TrackNet detector zoo
- **what:** Retrain or add a TOTNet-family detector using its MIT-licensed code (github.com/AugustRushG/TOTNet), visibility-weighted BCE loss (4 visibility levels incl. explicit full-occlusion signal) and mean-pixel occlusion augmentation, targeting our recall gap specifically
- **evidence:** On fully-occluded tennis frames TOTNet(OF) cuts RMSE from WASB's 264.45 to 15.31 and raises accuracy 0.33-0.67 vs WASB's near-zero; on its own new TTA table-tennis dataset, fully-occluded RMSE drops 37.30->7.19 and accuracy rises 0.63->0.80 (arXiv 2508.09650, MIT license)
- **expected_gain:** Directly targets our stated #1 weakness (recall, misses ~40%) with a technique proven specifically on occlusion cases in adjacent racket sports; realistic gain plausibly several recall points if occlusion (paddle/player) is a meaningful share of our misses
- **cost:** Moderate: requires re-implementing/retraining on our detector inputs; paper's own training used 2x A100 40GB, batch 16, 30 epochs -- fits our single A100 budget with adjusted batch size; inference drops to 12-28 FPS (offline-only, which matches our cloud-processing model)
- **risk:** No pickleball validation exists; occlusion-aug alone (without the weighted loss) was shown in the paper's own ablation to WORSEN results (RMSE 29.57->54.26), so the full recipe must be adopted together, not piecemeal; unclear how much of our recall loss is occlusion-driven vs. other causes (small/fast ball, motion blur, out-of-frame)
- **confidence:** med

### 2. Bootstrap in-domain pickleball training data via semi-supervised teacher-student pseudo-labeling using our existing ensemble as 'teacher' on owner's incoming unlabeled game footage
- **what:** Apply the Vandeghen et al. (CVPR 2022 workshop) confidence-doubt-weighted pseudo-labeling recipe (BSD-3, github.com/rvandeghen/SST): run current WASB+BlurBall+TrackNet ensemble as teacher over large volumes of unlabeled owner-captured video, train a student on labeled+pseudo-labeled data, iterate
- **evidence:** On SoccerNet-v3, this recipe improved mAP from 18.1% (teacher, 1% labeled) to 26.2% (best student) at just 193 labeled frames + ~55k unlabeled frames, and every labeled-data regime (1/5/10/100%) showed gains, plus a 2nd training iteration compounded further (39.5%->43.7%->45.1% at 10% labeled) (arXiv 2204.06859)
- **expected_gain:** Could meaningfully reduce the labeling burden for the owner's captures -- turns 'scarce in-domain data' from a hard blocker into a soft one by leveraging large volumes of cheap unlabeled video alongside a small labeled seed set; this is our single MEASURED binding constraint per the brief, so any credible mitigation is high-value
- **cost:** Low-to-moderate: reuses existing detector ensemble as teacher, no new architecture; needs a confidence-threshold sweep and an iterative training loop, all within single-A100 budget
- **risk:** Method cannot fix false negatives already missing from teacher pseudo-labels (paper's own caveat) -- if our ensemble systematically misses certain pickleball-specific patterns (fast net-play, paddle occlusion), pseudo-labels will silently inherit that recall gap rather than fix it; unproven on pickleball specifically
- **confidence:** med

### 3. Add a synthetic-physics-only 3D uplift/spin network (TT4D/'Where Is The Ball'/Uplifting-Table-Tennis style transformer) trained on simulated pickleball trajectories, requiring zero real 3D ground truth
- **what:** Build a MuJoCo/Unity-PhysX synthetic pickleball-trajectory generator (court dims + ball physics + paddle-impact model) and train a transformer or LSTM lifting network (à la TT4D/Uplifting Table Tennis) to convert our existing noisy 2D ensemble track into a true 3D mid-air trajectory plus paddle-impact inverse dynamics, replacing/augmenting our current arc solver
- **evidence:** 'Where Is The Ball' (arXiv 2506.05763): trained purely on synthetic PhysX data, zero-shot real tennis landing accuracy 87.21% vs SynthNet baseline 53.35%. TT4D (arXiv 2605.01234): 2.35cm 3D position error on synthetic table-tennis data, and racket pose/velocity recovered from ball trajectory ALONE (no racket tracker) with 26.4 deg / 0.58 m/s error on 92 real strokes, running >500 points/sec even on an old Titan X. Uplifting Table Tennis (arXiv 2511.20250, GPL-3.0): under half-FPS+missing-detection stress, retains 97.1% spin accuracy and 5.75px error vs a baseline's 67.7%/23.54px
- **expected_gain:** Directly fills our explicitly stated gap: 'NO true 3D mid-air ball positions... no spin estimation.' This is the single most direct match between published SOTA and one of our two named product gaps (3D lift + spin), and importantly needs ZERO real 3D pickleball ground truth to start, sidestepping the scarce-data constraint entirely for this sub-problem
- **cost:** High: requires building a pickleball-specific physics simulator (ball drag/Magnus + paddle-impact model; none exists off-the-shelf, unlike MuJoCo table-tennis assets reused across 3 papers) plus training a transformer from scratch; TT4D/Uplifting-TT code/datasets are only partially released (dataset promised, training code for lifting net not confirmed released)
- **risk:** Sim-to-real domain gap is sport-dependent and can be large ('Where Is The Ball' real IPL-soccer error 119cm vs 1.3cm synthetic, a ~90x gap, vs a much smaller gap for its tennis/mocap evals) -- pickleball's outdoor courts, wind, and paddle-material variability are unmodeled unknowns; no published work has attempted this for pickleball specifically; requires nontrivial physics-simulator engineering investment before any training can start
- **confidence:** low-med

### 4. Treat pb.vision/PlayReplay competitive intel as informative but not prescriptive; do not attempt to reverse-engineer a single-camera high-accuracy bar from them
- **what:** Recognize that pb.vision publishes zero technical details (closed SPA, no benchmark) and that the sport's most prominent live 2026 commercial deployment (PlayReplay, backed by Pickleball Inc.) explicitly uses FOUR net-post cameras, not one -- treat 'single camera 3D done very accurately' as an unverified competitor claim rather than a proven-achievable bar to match immediately
- **evidence:** PB Vision: no architecture/accuracy numbers found anywhere (help.pb.vision, pb.vision SPA). PlayReplay: 4-camera net-post system (pickleball.com and playreplay.io press releases, cross-confirmed by two independent primary sources), ITF Silver line-calling classification only claimed for its TENNIS deployments, no pickleball accuracy numbers yet. Hawk-Eye: 6+ cameras. Zenniz: 4 cameras + 30 audio sensors, explicitly because video-alone was judged insufficient
- **expected_gain:** Risk-management value: prevents over-indexing roadmap ambition on an unverified competitor accuracy claim (pb.vision) when the sport's better-funded, more scrutinized live system still uses multi-camera hardware -- recalibrates what 'good enough' single-camera 3D may realistically look like near-term
- **cost:** Low -- this is a framing/prioritization decision, not an engineering investment
- **risk:** pb.vision may still be quietly achieving strong single-camera 3D that we simply cannot verify; absence of evidence isn't evidence of absence, so this should inform confidence calibration, not roadmap paralysis
- **confidence:** high

### 5. Do NOT repeat direct supervised fine-tuning of TrackNet/WASB weights on a small single-match owner-captured pickleball dataset as the primary data strategy
- **what:** Avoid re-running the same 'label one match, fine-tune final layers, evaluate on same match' pattern that both the University of Rochester TrackNetV2 project and our own 4 prior internal fine-tunes already used and that measurably underperformed or inverted held-out accuracy
- **evidence:** University of Rochester's own disclosed methodology (the more authoritative source, cross-checked against a less rigorous secondary blog claiming much higher numbers) shows only 53%->65% TPR after fine-tuning 12,000 labeled frames from ONE match, with explicitly NO cross-match validation -- consistent with, not contradicting, our own 4 measured internal fine-tune failures/inversions. Roboflow's RF-DETR demo used only 118 images total (aggregate, non-ball-isolated metrics), too small to be informative either way
- **expected_gain:** Avoids wasting further engineering cycles repeating an approach with convergent external evidence (2 independent small-data attempts) of poor generalization; redirects effort toward semi-supervised/synthetic augmentation options above
- **cost:** None -- this is a negative/avoidance recommendation
- **risk:** If the owner's upcoming captures are large and diverse (multi-match, multi-camera-angle, multi-skill-level) rather than single-match, the failure mode observed in these small external examples may not apply -- data SCALE and DIVERSITY, not fine-tuning per se, may be the actual variable that was under-tested everywhere including our own attempts
- **confidence:** med

## Open questions

- What is PB Vision's (our named competitor's) actual model architecture, training-data scale, and measured accuracy? No technical disclosure was found anywhere (SPA site, help center, patents) — the only patent found (US11,615,540B2) belongs to an unrelated cricket-analytics company (Maiden AI) and should not be treated as PB Vision's actual method.
- Does IRLS-based robust regression on ballistic arc segments (our current solver) outperform or underperform the nonlinear-least-squares (TT3D, CasADi/IPOPT) or pure-transformer-lifting (TT4D) approaches used in published table-tennis work, specifically under our detector noise profile (hidden-FP 0.021, p95 34px)? No external paper benchmarks IRLS against these alternatives for this exact task.
- Would a teacher-student semi-supervised pipeline (Vandeghen et al. recipe) seeded by our existing WASB/BlurBall/TrackNet ensemble as the 'teacher,' applied to the owner's soon-to-arrive unlabeled pickleball footage, out-perform direct supervised fine-tuning on the same (small) labeled set -- given that our 4 prior direct-fine-tune attempts failed?
- Is the ~65% TPR ceiling seen in the University of Rochester single-match TrackNetV2 fine-tune (12,000 frames, no cross-match validation) representative of what any single-match fine-tune will hit on pickleball, or was that specific to their data/architecture choices -- i.e., is more data the fix, or is there an architecture-level reason (small/fast ball, paddle occlusion, court variety) that caps single-match fine-tunes regardless of scale?
- Does Meta's SAM3/3.1 concept-segmentation actually perform well on a small, fast, motion-blurred pickleball ball, given zero sports-ball-specific benchmarks were found and CVAT's own integration (as of Mar 2026) still lacks video-level mask propagation across a rally?
- Would the 'Where Is The Ball' canonical ray-plane synthetic-only 3D-lifting technique (zero real 3D training data required) actually generalize to pickleball's court geometry and bounce dynamics, given its own real-world results show large sport-dependent domain gaps (soccer 119cm vs 3.83cm mocap-tennis-like real error)? Code/data were still unreleased at last check.
- What GPU/runtime budget would a from-scratch TOTNet-style occlusion-aware retraining (2x A100 40GB per its GitHub README) actually require on our single-A100 setup, and is that compatible with our current cloud-processing cost/time budget?
- Is there a safe, license-clear path to reuse the existing pickleball-specific artifacts (Roboflow 118-image dataset, unlicensed TrackNet-Pickleball weights) as a warm-start, or do the missing/ambiguous licenses (no LICENSE file on TrackNet-Pickleball) block that even under an internal-use-only policy?
