# SOTA research: High-fidelity 6-DOF paddle/racket pose estimation from monocular sports video (+ hand-object interaction)

Generated 2026-07-05 by manager research workflow (28 agents). Adversarial-verification verdicts inline ([VERIFIED] markers + corrections applied).

## Scope and grounding

No published work targets 6-DOF pickleball paddle (or any racket/paddle) pose estimation using the current generation (2024-2026) of foundation-pose techniques. The single racket-specific 6D-pose paper found predates this generation by five years and uses classical stereo triangulation + plane fitting (table-tennis robots, 2019, https://www.researchgate.net/publication/336469630_Real-time_6D_Racket_Pose_Estimation_and_Classification_for_Table_Tennis_Robots — this source could not be independently re-fetched to verify details, flagged as unverified/failed-fetch). Everything below is therefore evidence assembled from adjacent domains: generic 6D object-pose foundation models, hand/hand-object reconstruction, 2D racket-pose benchmarks (table tennis/tennis/badminton), physics-informed racket-from-ball inference, and motion-blur-as-signal work. Findings that survived adversarial verification are marked [VERIFIED]; where a verdict came back "partially" I've applied the stated correction.

## 1. Generic 6-DOF object-pose foundation models

FoundationPose (NVIDIA, CVPR2024 Highlight) unifies model-based (CAD given) and model-free (≈16 posed reference images) 6D pose estimation and tracking, requiring no per-object fine-tuning at test time, and claimed #1 on the BOP leaderboard for model-based novel objects as of 2024/03 (https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.08344, https://github.com/NVlabs/FoundationPose) [VERIFIED, with the correction that the "#1" claim is a dated snapshot since surpassed by newer BOP entrants like FreeZeV2.1, Co-op, and 3PT-Pose]. Per-object pose estimation costs ~1.3s on an RTX 3090 (init 4ms + refine 0.88s + select 0.42s), with ~32Hz tracking thereafter once initialized. Code is released under the **NVIDIA Source Code License** — a restrictive, non-standard research license distinct from the CC-BY-NC terms we've already cleared for other assets; this needs separate legal review before use even internally.

SAM-6D (CVPR2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.15707) and FreeZe/FreeZeV2 (https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.09784) both require RGB-D input, a hard mismatch for our monocular consumer-camera setup. GigaPose (CVPR2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.14155) and MegaPose (CoRL2022, https://arxiv.org/abs/2212.06870) need CAD models and target coarse-pose speed rather than accuracy on thin objects. FoundPose (Meta, ECCV2024) is RGB-only and model-based (CAD template + DINOv2 patch matching, no learned matching network), released under CC BY-NC 4.0, but only the coarse-pose stage is public (refinement withheld), and no FPS/latency numbers were retrievable (https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.18809). OnePose++ (NeurIPS2022, Apache-2.0) eliminates the CAD requirement by reconstructing a semi-dense point-cloud model from a short reference video, explicitly targeting low-texture objects via detector-free LoFTR-style matching — architecturally attractive since our paddle has a textured face and is a known rigid template, but hard accuracy numbers weren't extractable from available sources, and training needs 8 GPUs at ~23GB VRAM each (https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.07673).

**The single most load-bearing piece of evidence for this whole category is negative and specific to handheld objects.** The BOP Challenge 2024 added BOP-H3 (HOT3D, HOPEv2, HANDAL) specifically to test real-world hand-held/egocentric scenarios; HANDAL's 40 objects (hammers, spatulas, whisks, etc.) are the closest public proxy to a graspable, planar-ish tool. On the live HANDAL leaderboard, the GigaPose lineage scores only **0.041–0.256 AP**, versus 0.42–0.60 AP on textureless-but-bulky ITODD industrial parts, and the best entrant overall is an unfamiliar 2025 method (3PT-Pose, 0.657 AP, 14.5s/image) — not FoundationPose, SAM-6D, or FoundPose, none of which even appear on this table (https://bop.felk.cvut.cz/leaderboards/pose-detection-unseen-bop24/handal/). The official BOP2024 report confirms model-free 6D localization on BOP-H3 (the exact "no-CAD, hand-tracked object" task shape) received **zero submissions** in 2024, and 2D detection of unseen objects remains ~35% behind seen-object detection (https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.02812). This strongly cautions against assuming FoundationPose/SAM-6D's headline "#1 on BOP" numbers transfer to a small, thin, planar, frequently hand-occluded paddle — the aggregate scores are driven by bulkier tabletop objects.

A February 2026 survey on monocular RGB 6D pose for AR generically discusses lighting/occlusion/clutter challenges and RGB's lack of explicit depth, but the more specific "RGB is markedly less robust than RGB-D in low-texture/low-light" wording was not substantiated verbatim in the retrievable text — treat that specific framing as unverified/over-paraphrased, though the broader spirit is corroborated elsewhere in the pose literature (https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10055-026-01315-4) [PARTIALLY corrected: also mis-dated as "2025" in the original claim; it is a 2026 publication].

## 2. Hand pose and hand-object interaction

WiLoR (CVPR2025, our already-queued asset) is a two-stage detector+MANO-transformer pipeline achieving FreiHAND PA-MPJPE 5.5mm / HO3D PA-MPJPE 7.5mm, with a detector running 138–175 FPS on an RTX 4090 (45x faster than prior SOTA ContactHands) and explicit design for smoothness **without any temporal component** — smoothness emerges from strong per-frame accuracy plus training-data diversity (2M+ in-the-wild WHIM images for the detector; 4.2M images across 14 datasets for the reconstruction transformer) (https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.12259, https://github.com/rolpotamias/WiLoR) [VERIFIED, with the correction that the 2M and 4.2M figures apply to two different sub-modules, not one combined figure]. Its license, CC-BY-NC-ND, is confirmed compatible with our internal-use-only constraint exactly as assumed.

HaMeR (CVPR2024, https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.05251) and its 2026 distilled variant Fast-HaMeR emphasize scale over architecture novelty and are robust to occlusion by construction. Hamba (NeurIPS2024) reports FreiHAND PA-MPVPE 5.3mm using a Mamba state-space block with 88.5% fewer tokens than attention (https://arxiv.org/abs/2407.09646). HaWoR (CVPR2025 Highlight) adds egocentric SLAM + motion-infilling for occlusion/out-of-frame recovery (https://arxiv.org/abs/2501.02973) — relevant if paddle/hand leaves frame briefly.

For **joint** hand-object reconstruction: HOLD (CVPR2024, MIT license) is category-agnostic and template-free but does per-video NeRF/SDF test-time optimization; a third-party estimate puts this at ~25 hours per 200-frame video on an RTX 4090 — almost certainly incompatible with our per-clip throughput needs (https://github.com/zc-alexfan/hold). HOISDF (CVPR2024) is feed-forward (single pass, no per-frame optimization), using a global signed-distance-field prior to constrain hand-object contact, reaching DexYCB MJE 10.1mm / OCE 18.4mm vs prior SOTA 12.6mm/27.3mm (https://arxiv.org/html/2402.17062v1) — architecturally the best fit for our deployability constraint among joint methods, but its GitHub repo has **no LICENSE file** (effectively all-rights-reserved) and it's trained/evaluated on generic YCB objects grasped mostly statically, not a fast/thin/planar paddle. MOHO (CVPR2024) explicitly targets hand-induced occlusion via synthetic multi-view occlusion-free pretraining + real-data fine-tuning, running 10 FPS on an A100 (vs. 23 FPS for a comparable non-occlusion-aware baseline, IHOI) (https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11696) — also no license file. EasyHOI (CVPR2025) and HandOS (Dec 2024, single-stage) are newer successors in this lineage but were not deep-read in this pass.

A directly analogous small-object-in-hand study, "6D Pose Estimation on Spoons and Hands" (May 2025), chains Grounding DINO+SAM2 segmentation, VOS mask propagation, UniDepth monocular depth, and BundleSDF tracking, entirely zero-shot with no fine-tuning. Its central lesson: **segmentation-mask quality on the small handheld object itself is the binding constraint** — SAM2 achieves spoon-region Dice 0.9286 vs. hand-region 0.8255, but BundleSDF loses/corrupts pose after full occlusion (spoon disappearing behind hand/mouth), producing incorrect boxes or orientation discontinuities on re-emergence (https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02335). This is a strong cautionary analog for any pipeline (ours included) that depends on clean paddle masks through hand-occlusion.

## 3. Racket/paddle-specific 2D pose work (nearest available proxy)

RacketVision (AAAI2026 Oral, MIT license, code+data at github.com/OrcustD/RacketVision) is the newest, most topically adjacent benchmark: 1,672 clips / 435,179 frames across table tennis, tennis, badminton, with a unified 5-keypoint racket schema (Top, Bottom, Handle, Left, Right) via RTMDet+RTMPose. It reports PCK@0.2 of 81.8%/89.6%/88.5% and MPJPE of 9.71px/5.34px/5.00px for table tennis/tennis/badminton respectively, but **side/edge keypoints (which govern face-width/orientation) drop to only 64.8–80.1% PCK@0.2** vs. 92.6–99.7% for structural (long-axis) points, attributed explicitly to hand-grip occlusion and motion blur — directly mirroring our own weak axis (paddle-face orientation) (https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.17045) [VERIFIED, including confirmation that the method is purely 2D pixel-space, no 3D/6-DOF attempted]. The paper also found that naively concatenating racket-pose features into a downstream ball-trajectory model *hurts* performance versus a ball-only baseline — a caution against naive feature fusion, relevant to how we might feed a future keypoint signal into our fusion.

Older/adjacent racket-tracking work: the 2019 table-tennis stereo system (unverifiable re-fetch); golf clubhead tracking historically used global 2D motion models, more recently HRNet+DeepLabCut-style combos (https://ideausher.com/blog/golf-app-development-boost-engagement-pose-detection/, 2024, secondary source); SwingVision and related USPTO patents (e.g., US10482319) describe proprietary deep-learning racket tracking but disclose no architecture/accuracy detail usable as technical evidence; Theia Markerless's 2025 baseball bat tracker explicitly uses **multiple synchronized cameras**, not monocular — a precedent that doesn't transfer to our single-camera constraint (https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/theia-launches-worlds-first-ai-powered-markerless-bat-and-body-tracking...).

## 4. Physics/impact-based constraints (relevant to our dormant ball-reflection factor)

TT4D (2026) is the closest published validation of exactly our dormant idea: it recovers racket orientation and velocity at ball impact purely by inverting a reconstructed 3D ball trajectory via inverse optimal control (single-shooting RK4 + Magnus-effect model), reporting **26.4±4.4° orientation error and 0.58±0.40 m/s velocity error** against motion-capture ground truth across 92 real strokes at 3.72 m/s average impact speed, with residual error concentrated in unmodeled racket restitution properties (https://arxiv.org/html/2605.01234) [VERIFIED]. This is a concrete existence proof and target error-bound for our own reflection factor, contingent entirely on the separate ball-tracking chain first delivering reliable 3D mid-air velocities — which per our own state tracking is not yet unlocked. Related spin/impact-model work (SpinDOE, event-camera spin estimation, black-box-vs-gray-box trajectory prediction) reinforces that even direct racket-adjacent physics inference is imperfect (e.g., IMU-based forward-spin classification only 79.4% accurate) and that motion blur specifically corrupts logo/visual spin-tracking methods (https://arxiv.org/pdf/2404.09870).

## 5. Differentiable-rendering / silhouette refinement

Diff-DOPE (NVIDIA, nvdiffrast-based render-and-compare refiner, not a from-scratch estimator) costs **up to 3.5s per single object per frame** (32 parallel hypotheses × 100 iterations, 34ms/iteration) on an RTX 4090 — two to three orders of magnitude too slow for per-frame use at rally framerate, though accuracy is largely resolution-insensitive (87.9 vs. 87.7 AUC on HOPE at 100% vs. 25% resolution), suggesting a cheaper configuration is possible (https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00463) [VERIFIED]. This rules out per-frame differentiable refinement but leaves open a sparse, keyframe-only application (e.g., at suspected impact frames) as a plausible use of nvdiffrast/our template mesh.

## 6. Motion blur as signal

BlurBall (2025, CC BY-SA 4.0) jointly detects a table-tennis ball and its motion-blur parameters via PCA over a thresholded blur mask (blur angle MAE 6.8±18.9°, 79 FPS) — but this only works because a ball is rotationally symmetric, giving one unambiguous axis. RacketVision (2025/2026), the newest racket-pose paper, does **not** use blur direction as a signal anywhere despite explicitly flagging blur as a challenge for its hardest keypoints — confirming that adapting a blur-axis cue to a planar/elongated paddle (2-3 rotational DOF, not 1) would be genuinely novel work, not an established recipe (https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.18387, https://arxiv.org/html/2511.17045v3) [VERIFIED for the RacketVision-does-not-use-blur claim].

## 7. Unexplored, low-cost engineering path

An identified gap with no literature precedent but well-established constituent primitives: extracting an oriented quadrilateral from our already-owned, currently-unused YOLO paddle-segmentation checkpoint's mask, then solving planar PnP via IPPE (BSD-3-Clause, 4-point coplanar solve, 50-80x faster than general solvers for rectangular targets) (https://github.com/tobycollins/IPPE). This reuses an idle asset for a cheap, interpretable, physically-grounded orientation signal independent of the finger-joint/box fusion, though mask reliability under motion blur/occlusion near impact (our highest-value moments) is unproven — the spoon-tracking analog suggests segmentation quality is the binding constraint in this class of problem (https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02335).

## Cross-cutting constraints for our situation

- **Single consumer/broadcast-style monocular camera, 3-15m range**: rules out RGB-D methods (SAM-6D, FreeZe) and multi-camera precedents (Theia, some table-tennis stereo systems).
- **Cloud A100 40GB, need per-clip/per-rally throughput, not single-image demos**: rules out per-video test-time optimization (HOLD, ~25h/200-frame video) and per-frame differentiable refinement (Diff-DOPE, 3.5s/frame); favors feed-forward methods (HOISDF, MOHO, WiLoR, RTMPose-style keypoint detectors).
- **Internal-use-only, NC licenses acceptable**: WiLoR (CC-BY-NC-ND), FoundPose (CC-BY-NC-4.0), RacketVision (MIT), OnePose++ (Apache-2.0), IPPE (BSD-3-Clause), HOLD (MIT) are all clear. FoundationPose's NVIDIA Source Code License is non-standard and needs explicit legal review distinct from prior NC clearances. HOISDF and MOHO have **no license file at all** — a harder blocker than any NC license, since "no license" defaults to all-rights-reserved.
- **Scarce in-domain pickleball data**: every foundation-pose/hand-object method surveyed is trained on generic tabletop/household/YCB-style objects or other racket sports, never pickleball; the BOP-H3/HANDAL evidence specifically shows this generation of models does *not* transfer well to thin/handheld objects even within its own training distribution, so in-domain paddle data (CVAT labels, keypoint labels) remains the most reliably load-bearing lever regardless of which architecture is chosen.


## Ranked options (synthesis agent; manager re-derives decisions)

### 1. A. Paddle keypoint-detector fine-tune (RacketVision-style 5-point 2D schema) fused with existing SAM-3D palm-frame + PnP
- **what:** Fine-tune a lightweight top-down keypoint head (RTMPose-style, or reuse our idle YOLO segmentation checkpoint's mask to derive Top/Bottom/Handle/Left/Right points) on CVAT-labeled paddle boxes+corners, feed as richer 2D evidence into the existing SLERP fusion instead of a plain bounding box.
- **evidence:** RacketVision (AAAI2026, MIT license) proves this exact recipe works across 3 racket sports at PCK@0.2 81.8-89.6% with off-the-shelf RTMDet+RTMPose (https://arxiv.org/abs/2511.17045) [VERIFIED]; its own failure mode (side/edge keypoints 64.8-80.1% due to occlusion/blur) is precisely our paddle-face-orientation problem, so it's a realistic, not inflated, expectation.
- **expected_gain:** Likely improves orientation/IoU beyond current 0.24-0.34 IoU by adding 2-3 more constrained 2D points (long axis + face width) vs. a single box; directly targets our stated weak axis (absolute pronation).
- **cost:** Low — reuses labeled data infra (CVAT boxes already exist, need corner/keypoint labels added), architecture is small and fast (real-time-class), fits comfortably on A100.
- **risk:** Side/edge keypoints are the hardest part per RacketVision's own numbers, so gains on face-normal/orientation specifically may be smaller than gains on long-axis heading.
- **confidence:** high

### 2. B. Foundation zero-shot 6D pose model (FoundationPose / SAM-6D / FoundPose) via model-free scan of our physical paddle
- **what:** Capture ~16+ reference images (or short video) of our actual paddle, fit the model-free pipeline, run per-frame/tracking 6D pose.
- **evidence:** FoundationPose model-free mode is architecturally compatible with our 'known template' paddle and runs at ~32Hz tracking on comparable GPUs (https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.08344) [VERIFIED]; BUT on the one BOP dataset made of handheld/graspable tools (HANDAL), this generation of models (GigaPose lineage) scores only 0.04-0.26 AP vs 0.5-0.9 on bulkier/tabletop objects, and model-free 6D localization on BOP-H3 had ZERO submissions in 2024 (https://bop.felk.cvut.cz/leaderboards/pose-detection-unseen-bop24/handal/, https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.02812) — meaning the headline 'aggregate #1' claims for FoundationPose/SAM-6D do not have validated evidence on thin/handheld objects like a paddle.
- **expected_gain:** Uncertain — could give true 6-DOF (not palm+box heuristic) if it transfers, but no benchmark evidence supports transfer to thin/planar/hand-occluded objects; more likely to underperform our current fused approach out of the box.
- **cost:** Medium-high — reference-image capture/rig setup, legal review for NVIDIA Source Code License (restrictive, non-standard even for internal-only use) or CC-BY-NC-4.0 (FoundPose), integration engineering.
- **risk:** High risk of disappointing accuracy given HANDAL evidence; license friction (NVIDIA Source Code License is unusually restrictive vs. our other NC/research licenses already accepted).
- **confidence:** low

### 3. C. Differentiable-render (Diff-DOPE style) refinement pass on sparse keyframes only (not per-frame)
- **what:** Use our existing paddle template mesh + nvdiffrast render-and-compare to refine pose at select high-value frames (e.g. suspected impact frames) rather than every frame.
- **evidence:** Diff-DOPE costs ~3.5s/object/frame on an RTX 4090 (32 hypotheses x 100 iters) — 2-3 orders of magnitude too slow for 30-60fps per-frame use, but resolution-insensitive (87.9 vs 87.7 AUC at 100% vs 25% res) so could be cheapened further (https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.00463) [VERIFIED].
- **expected_gain:** Could sharpen pose specifically at ball-impact frames (highest-value moments) without redesigning the whole per-frame pipeline.
- **cost:** Medium — needs nvdiffrast integration, GPU time budget per selected frame (~1-3s at reduced iteration count), no retraining needed (test-time only).
- **risk:** Not validated on planar/thin/fast objects; benefit is confined to the sparse frames chosen, doesn't fix general per-frame smoothness/jitter.
- **confidence:** medium

### 4. D. Segmentation-mask -> oriented-quadrilateral -> planar PnP (IPPE) using our unused YOLO paddle-seg checkpoint
- **what:** Extract paddle mask each frame, fit a minimum-area oriented rectangle/quadrilateral to the 4 corners, solve planar PnP (IPPE) using the known real-world paddle rectangle dimensions to get a direct 6-DOF estimate independent of hand/finger cues.
- **evidence:** IPPE (BSD-3-Clause) computes full 6-DOF pose from 4 coplanar points and its rectangle-specialized variant is 50-80x faster than general solvers (https://github.com/tobycollins/IPPE); this is a well-known classical-CV primitive, but no literature was found combining it specifically with an instance-segmentation-mask corner extractor for a small, motion-blurred, textured planar sports implement — an identified white-space/engineering gap, not an established recipe.
- **expected_gain:** Potentially large — reuses an already-owned, currently idle asset (YOLO seg checkpoint) for a cheap, interpretable, physically-grounded orientation signal that's independent of and complementary to the finger-joint/box fusion.
- **cost:** Low-medium — no new model training required if the seg checkpoint's mask quality is adequate; needs corner-extraction + PnP glue code and validation against CVAT ground truth.
- **risk:** Segmentation mask quality under motion blur/hand-occlusion is unproven for our paddle specifically (analogous eating-utensil paper found segmentation quality is the binding constraint, https://arxiv.org/abs/2505.02335); mask corners may be unreliable exactly when the paddle moves fastest (near impact) — the moments we care most about.
- **confidence:** medium

### 5. E. Upgrade hand-crop stage to WiLoR (already planned)
- **what:** Replace/supplement SAM-3D MHR70 finger joints with WiLoR's detector+MANO reconstruction for the palm-frame input to the fusion.
- **evidence:** WiLoR license (CC-BY-NC-ND) confirmed compatible with our internal-use-only constraint [VERIFIED]; detector runs 138-175 FPS on RTX4090 with strong occlusion/motion-blur-diverse training data (2M WHIM images) and per-frame smoothness without temporal modeling (https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.12259) [VERIFIED].
- **expected_gain:** Likely improves finger-joint/palm-frame fidelity (our known weak-rest-pose-bias axis) since WiLoR's training explicitly includes occlusion/motion-blur-diverse in-the-wild data vs. our current MHR70 source.
- **cost:** Low — matches an already-queued plan; mainly integration + benchmarking against current fused-estimator IoU/center-error/jitter bars.
- **risk:** WiLoR gives hand pose, not paddle pose directly — still requires the same constant-grip-transform assumption that MEASURED truths already show is the weak link (pronation bias is a body-model/rest-pose issue, not purely a hand-tracker-quality issue), so gains may be capped.
- **confidence:** medium

### 6. F. Physics/ball-reflection factor (activate once 3D ball velocities exist)
- **what:** Once the ball-tracking chain produces 3D mid-air velocities, activate the dormant ball-reflection factor (paddle normal from ball velocity change at impact), using TT4D's inverse-optimal-control approach as a template/target.
- **evidence:** TT4D demonstrates recovering racket orientation to 26.4±4.4° and velocity to 0.58±0.40 m/s purely from 3D ball trajectory inversion via IOC + a physics restitution model, on 92 real strokes (https://arxiv.org/html/2605.01234) [VERIFIED] — this is a concrete existence proof and error-bound target for our own dormant factor.
- **expected_gain:** Could meaningfully correct orientation specifically at/near impact frames — our hardest and highest-value moments — once the prerequisite (3D ball velocity chain) lands.
- **cost:** High and NOT current-cycle — blocked entirely on the separate ball-tracking chain producing reliable 3D mid-air velocities (per BALL chain state memory, that chain is still not unlocked). No cost to us right now beyond keeping the factor's interface ready.
- **risk:** TT4D's own residual error is concentrated in racket open/closed angle (X-axis rotation) due to unknown racket restitution properties — analogous unknowns exist for pickleball paddle-face materials and would need characterization.
- **confidence:** medium (contingent on an external blocker)

### 7. G. Joint hand-object feed-forward networks (HOISDF, MOHO) as an alternative to constant-grip-transform fusion
- **what:** Adopt a feed-forward joint hand+rigid-object pose network trained/fine-tuned toward a paddle-like object instead of hand-joints x constant-transform.
- **evidence:** HOISDF is feed-forward (single pass, no per-frame optimization) reaching DexYCB MJE 10.1mm/OCE 18.4mm (https://arxiv.org/html/2402.17062v1); MOHO explicitly targets hand-occlusion of the held object via synthetic multi-view supervision, running 10 FPS on an A100 (https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11696) — but both trained on bulkier, mostly-static YCB-style grasped objects, not a fast/thin/planar/motion-blurred paddle, and both repos have NO license file (effectively all-rights-reserved), unlike our other NC-licensed assets.
- **expected_gain:** Could unify hand+paddle estimation in one model rather than a fused pipeline, potentially reducing compounding errors from the constant-grip-transform assumption.
- **cost:** High — would require re-training/fine-tuning on in-domain (paddle) data we largely lack, plus legal clearance given missing licenses.
- **risk:** Domain mismatch (object size/speed/planarity) is large; license risk (no LICENSE file) is a hard blocker without legal sign-off distinct from our already-accepted NC licenses.
- **confidence:** low

- MOHO (CVPR2024) explicitly targets hand-induced occlusion of a held object via synthetic multi-view occlusion-free supervision + real-data fine-tuning, running at 10 FPS on an A100 (vs faster 23 FPS IHOI baseline); repo has no license file (https://arxiv.org/abs/2310.11696, https://github.com/ZhangCYG/MOHO)
- BlurBall (2025, CC BY-SA 4.0) recovers a ball's motion-blur direction via PCA over the thresholded blur mask (blur angle MAE 6.8±18.9°, length MAE 1.2±1.1px, 79 FPS), proving the recipe works but only for a rotationally-symmetric disc — giving a single ambiguous 1D axis, not the 2-3 rotational DOF a planar/elongated paddle needs (https://arxiv.org/abs/2509.18387)
- No paper (including RacketVision, Nov 2025, the newest racket-pose benchmark) uses motion blur as an orientation SIGNAL for an elongated implement — RacketVision treats blur purely as noise/challenge degrading its side-keypoint accuracy, confirming a blur-axis-as-orientation-cue approach for our paddle would be genuinely novel work, not prior art (https://arxiv.org/html/2511.17045v3)
- IPPE (Infinitesimal Plane-based Pose Estimation, BSD-3-Clause) computes full 6-DoF planar pose from as few as 4 coplanar point correspondences and its solveSquare variant runs 50-80x faster than general PnP for rectangular targets — directly applicable to a segmented paddle-mask's 4 corners, but no literature was found connecting a segmentation-mask corner-extractor to this primitive for small sports equipment specifically (https://github.com/tobycollins/IPPE)
- OnePose++ (NeurIPS2022/2023, Apache-2.0) builds a 6-DOF pose model for a novel rigid object from only a short reference RGB video (no CAD, no per-object retraining), targeting exactly low/rich-textured planar objects, but hard accuracy numbers were not extractable from the fetched sources and training needs 8 GPUs at ~23GB VRAM each (https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.07673)
- FreeZe/FreeZeV2 and SAM-6D require RGB-D input (fused geometric point-cloud + DINOv2 features), which is a mismatch for a monocular consumer-camera pickleball setup with no depth sensor (https://arxiv.org/abs/2506.09784, https://arxiv.org/abs/2311.15707)
- A 2026 survey on monocular RGB 6D pose for AR generically notes lighting/occlusion/background-clutter challenges and RGB's loss of depth information, but the specific quantified claims about RGB vs RGB-D robustness gaps in low-texture/low-light scenes were NOT substantiated verbatim in the source as retrieved — treat as a correction on an over-specific paraphrase (https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10055-026-01315-4)
- SwingVision and related USPTO patents (e.g. US10482319) describe proprietary deep-learning racket/implement tracking trained on large corpora, but exact architecture/accuracy is not disclosed in patent text — cannot be used as technical evidence, only as competitive-landscape signal (https://image-ppubs.uspto.gov/dirsearch-public/print/downloadPdf/10482319)
- Theia Markerless's 2025 bat-tracking product uses multiple synchronized high-speed cameras (not monocular) plus deep-learning keypoint models to track full-body + bat 3D trajectory — a multi-camera precedent, not applicable to our single-camera constraint (https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/theia-launches-worlds-first-ai-powered-markerless-bat-and-body-tracking...)

## Open questions

- Does our owned YOLO paddle-segmentation checkpoint produce mask quality good enough (especially near ball impact / fastest motion) to support a corner-extraction + IPPE planar-PnP pipeline (Option D)? No literature validates this combination for a small sports implement — would need our own pilot test.
- Would a blur-axis-as-orientation-signal (BlurBall-style PCA on the paddle's blur mask) actually disambiguate 2-3 rotational DOF for a planar/elongated object, or only recover the same single ambiguous heading axis it gives for round balls? This is unestablished in any published work (2024-2026 racket-pose literature does not attempt it).
- What is FoundationPose/SAM-6D/FoundPose's real accuracy specifically on a small, thin, PLANAR, textured, fast-moving object like a paddle, given that the closest public proxy (BOP HANDAL, handheld tools) shows this model family scoring only 0.04-0.26 AP — would need our own pilot rather than trusting BOP-Classic-Core aggregate numbers.
- Is the NVIDIA Source Code License (FoundationPose) actually acceptable for our 'internal-use-only, NC-licenses-usable' policy, or does its specific restrictive terms (beyond standard CC-BY-NC) require separate legal review? Not resolved by any source in this research pass.
- Given HOISDF and MOHO both have NO LICENSE file on GitHub (effectively all-rights-reserved), is any use — even internal research — permissible without direct outreach to the authors? This differs from our previously-cleared CC-BY-NC/CC-BY-NC-ND assets and needs an explicit legal call.
- How much in-domain pickleball paddle CVAT-labeled data (beyond the existing 2 scoring clips) would be needed to fine-tune a RacketVision-style keypoint detector (Option A) to a useful accuracy, given RacketVision itself needed 24,621 annotations across 435,179 frames for 3 sports?
- When (if ever) will the ball-tracking chain deliver reliable 3D mid-air ball velocities, which is the hard prerequisite for activating the dormant ball-reflection factor (Option F) and for testing whether TT4D's 26.4±4.4 degree/0.58±0.40 m/s error bound is achievable in our pickleball setting (different implement size/mass/restitution than table tennis)?
