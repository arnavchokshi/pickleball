# SOTA research: Temporally-stable, world-grounded 3D human mesh recovery from monocular sports video (eliminating jitter and foot-sliding)

Generated 2026-07-05 by manager research workflow (29 agents). Adversarial-verification verdicts inline ([VERIFIED] markers + corrections applied).

## Scope and framing

The question for us is narrow but consequential: our production stack runs Meta's SAM 3D Body (SAM-3D-Body / MHR70) per-frame on an A100, with all temporal coherence, world-grounding, and anti-jitter behavior implemented as classical post-processing (tracking, homography placement, stance-phase foot pinning, stance-aware root smoothing, one-euro joint smoothing). The owner's ruling is that SAM-3D-Body is the only approved skeleton path unless a replacement shows *measurably* better temporal stability and world grounding. This report surveys the 2024-2026 literature on (1) video-native world-grounded HMR that could replace our whole architecture, (2) learned or classical denoisers/refiners bolted onto a frozen per-frame model like ours, and (3) physics-based refinement — and flags where our specific constraints (single consumer camera, far/small subjects, handheld drift, 4 simultaneous players, NC-license-acceptable internal use) bite each candidate.

## 1. Video-native world-grounded HMR: the "replace the whole stack" candidates

**GVHMR** (SIGGRAPH Asia 2024, extended TPAMI 2026) is architecturally the most interesting single-person alternative. Instead of autoregressively integrating a global trajectory frame-to-frame (which accumulates drift), it estimates pose per-frame in a "Gravity-View" (GV) coordinate frame defined jointly by gravity direction and the instantaneous camera-view direction, then composes frames into world coordinates using camera rotations from a SLAM/VO front end (default DPVO). This mechanism claim was independently checked against the primary text and confirmed accurate [VERIFIED] (https://arxiv.org/abs/2409.06662, https://github.com/zju3dv/GVHMR). On reported world-grounded metrics, GVHMR beats WHAM across the board: RICH foot-sliding 3.0mm vs WHAM's 3.3mm and jitter 12.8 vs 19.7; EMDB foot-sliding 3.5mm vs 4.4mm and jitter 16.7 vs 22.5, with W-MPJPE100 roughly 25-30% lower on both datasets [VERIFIED mechanism; metrics not independently re-verified beyond primary-source table check] (https://zju3dv.github.io/gvhmr). Its core network processes a 1430-frame (~45s) clip in 0.28s on an RTX 4090. Critically, GVHMR is **non-causal**: it needs the whole clip's camera trajectory computed upfront via SLAM/VO before the pose network runs — the paper's "parallel inference for infinitely long sequences" claim is a batching/throughput property, not true streaming operation. This is compatible with our existing per-clip offline batch processing but would preclude any future real-time/live requirement. License: a custom ZJU non-commercial research/education license, forbidding commercial use without contacting the authors and requiring derivatives to stay open-source (https://github.com/zju3dv/GVHMR/blob/main/LICENSE) — acceptable under our "internal R&D research licenses are usable" policy.

**WHAM** (CVPR 2024) is the genuinely causal alternative: a uni-directional RNN that predicts motion online, frame-by-frame, without needing future context, fusing 2D-keypoint-lifted motion priors (trained on AMASS) with video image features and a predicted foot-contact probability used to correct trajectory drift. Its core network runs ~200fps on an A100 (our GPU class), but the *full* pipeline including 2D-pose/SLAM preprocessing drops to ~9fps online (~49fps batched) — preprocessing dominates cost, echoing our own finding that data plumbing (not inference) dominates our BODY-stage time (https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.07531, https://github.com/yohanshin/WHAM). One claim required correction after adversarial check: the original data mislabeled SLAHMR's 326.9mm and GLAMR's 280.8mm as W-MPJPE100 when they are actually WA-MPJPE100 (true W-MPJPE100 for those baselines is 776.1mm/726.6mm), and WHAM's tabulated jitter value of "22.5" is in units of 10 m/s³ (~225 m/s³ true value), not 22.5 m/s³ as originally stated — WHAM still legitimately beats both baselines on WA-MPJPE100 (135.6mm) [claim CORRECTED, underlying comparison still holds] (https://arxiv.org/abs/2312.07531). WHAM's code is MIT-licensed, but it functionally requires the NC-licensed SMPL body model to run, and it emits 24-joint SMPL, not our MHR-70+full-mesh format.

**TRAM** (ECCV 2024) tackles our handheld-camera-drift problem head-on: a "masked" DROID-SLAM (masking dynamic-human regions from both RGB input and bundle-adjustment) recovers metric-scale camera trajectory, cutting camera Absolute Trajectory Error from 2.42m (unmasked) to 0.32m (masked, GT scale) / 0.66m (masked, estimated scale) on EMDB — a concrete, ablated demonstration that masking people out of SLAM matters for moving-camera footage (https://arxiv.org/abs/2403.17346). TRAM also cuts EMDB Root Translation Error to 3.1mm vs WHAM's 8.4mm. Code is MIT but bundles a modified DROID-SLAM submodule and requires the NC SMPL license; no A100/latency numbers are published anywhere in the paper.

**PromptHMR / PromptHMR-Vid** (CVPR 2025) targets a different piece of our problem: small/far/crowded subjects. Its full-image (not per-crop) transformer with spatial (box/mask) and semantic (text) prompts is explicitly evaluated on multi-person interaction benchmarks (HI4D/CHI3D), closer to our 4-player-court density than typical single-person benchmarks, though still only validated at 2-person density. On EMDB-2 world-coordinate evaluation, PromptHMR-Vid reports the best-of-four numbers among itself/WHAM/TRAM/GVHMR: Jitter 16.3 (×10 m/s³) and Foot-Skating 3.5mm (https://arxiv.org/abs/2504.06397). No inference-speed numbers are reported anywhere in the paper — a real gap for GPU-budget planning. License: Meshcapade's non-commercial "Scientific Research Use Only," plus a separate SMPL-X license requirement.

**OnlineHMR** (CVPR 2026, arXiv:2603.17355) is the closest published match to "single causal model with world grounding baked in," using a causal key-value cache plus human-centric incremental SLAM. However it runs at only 3.3 FPS (~0.30s per-frame delay) on an RTX 6000 Ada — far slower than our 15.3ms/person-frame SAM-3D-Body budget — and the authors explicitly state it "struggles with abrupt camera switches or multi-camera inputs."

**Common gap across all four**: none were evaluated on footage resembling ours — elevated/far camera, small in-frame subjects, 4 simultaneous players, handheld drift. All benchmarks are 3DPW/EMDB/RICH/HI4D, which are pedestrian-scale, mostly single- or two-person, ground-level-camera datasets. This is the single largest evidentiary gap for Option E in our ranking.

## 2. Same-domain evidence: broadcast-sports pipelines that already look like ours

The single most directly relevant data point in this search is **SMART** (arXiv:2605.31551, 2026 FIFA WorldPose Skeletal Tracking Challenge submission), which is architecturally near-identical to our stack: a per-frame SMPL-family mesh model (SMPLest-X, fine-tuned) → RAFT optical-flow camera tracking with MAD outlier filtering → foot-plane ground anchoring → two-pass classical smoothing, evaluated on real 1920×1080/50fps broadcast soccer video. It reaches held-out test Global MPJPE 0.324m / Local MPJPE 0.054m, improving the FIFA baseline score from 1.053 to 0.647 (38.6%). Critically, **SMART explicitly tried a learned sliding-window temporal-refinement network and rejected it**: it improved their own validation set by 22% but degraded held-out competition performance due to over-smoothing, so the final submission reverted to plain Gaussian(σ=1.5-3 frames)+MAD smoothing. This is same-domain (broadcast sports, elevated/moving camera) evidence that a classical-smoothing architecture like ours is not obviously inferior to a learned temporal net — a useful counterweight to the abstract appeal of Option E. RAFT-small+MAD camera tracking (0.041° rotation error at 55ms/frame) also beats homography/Lucas-Kanade baselines in their ablation and is a directly portable technique. SMART reports no code release.

## 3. Techniques built directly on top of SAM-3D-Body (frozen backbone)

Two very recent papers keep SAM-3D-Body/MHR frozen and add post-processing, matching our exact "don't retrain the backbone" constraint:

- **"World-Coordinate Human Motion Retargeting via SAM 3D Body"** (arXiv:2512.21573, Dec 2025) locks per-track shape/scale parameters for constant bone length, applies latent-space (not raw-joint) Charbonnier-smoothed sliding-window optimization on MHR's compact pose code, and uses a differentiable soft foot-ground contact model for world grounding — directly analogous to our stance-aware smoothing and foot-pinning stages. However it reports **zero quantitative metrics** (no MPJPE, jitter, or foot-slide numbers) and releases no code, so it can only serve as a design blueprint, not adoptable evidence (https://arxiv.org/abs/2512.21573).
- **SAM-Body4D** (arXiv:2512.08406, Dec 2025) is training-free and attacks the *input* side: SAM3 video-segmentation masklets plus occlusion-aware inpainting (Diffusion-VAS) give SAM-3D-Body temporally-consistent mask prompts instead of independently-detected boxes each frame — a plausible fix for input-driven jitter. Code is MIT-licensed at github.com/gaomingqi/sam-body4d, but again reports zero quantitative benchmarks, only qualitative figures.
- **Fast SAM 3D Body** (arXiv:2603.15603, Mar 2026) [VERIFIED as accurately described, self-reported only] is a training-free acceleration layer (parallelized crop extraction, pruned decoder, feedforward MHR→SMPL head replacing iterative fitting) claiming 8.3-10.9x speedup (~65ms/frame on RTX 5090) with a small MPJPE regression (54.8mm→59.5mm) but PA-MPJPE improvement (33.8mm→30.4mm) on 3DPW oracle protocol. No A100 numbers are published anywhere, so its benefit on our exact hardware is unverified; the paper's jitter-cause hypothesis (single-viewpoint self-occlusion during large rotations, fixed by multi-view fusion) is offered for a robotics-teleop use case, not validated against sports-video jitter. Code is MIT (https://github.com/yangtiming/Fast-SAM-3D-Body).

## 4. Multi-person / occlusion-robust tracking (our 4-player scenario)

**CoMotion** (Apple, ICLR 2025) uses tracking-by-attention with a per-track recurrent hidden state to maintain identity through occlusion without re-detection, reporting MOTA 71.4/IDF1 79.5 on PoseTrack21 vs 4DHumans' 56.7/70.9, at 176ms/frame on a V100 (12x faster than 4DHumans). It explicitly does **not** model camera motion or produce a shared world frame, and the authors admit "track collapse" (multiple tracks merging) and identity switches specifically in close-proximity multi-person scenes — precisely our doubles/close-net scenario. Code+weights are released under Apple's custom Sample Code License (non-standard, needs legal review even for internal use) (https://github.com/apple/ml-comotion).

**RAM** (CVPR 2026, arXiv:2603.19929) targets the same problem with adaptive Kalman-filtered identity association plus a memory-augmented temporal HMR module. After adversarial verification [tables ARE extractable, correcting an earlier claim they weren't; verdict PARTIALLY], RAM reports strong identity-switch robustness (PoseTrack18 IDs=15 vs 4DHumans' 349 and CoMotion's 344) and better 3DPW accuracy (MPJPE 53.0mm vs CoMotion's 60.0mm) — but is **not a clean sweep**: its own PoseTrack18 zero-shot MOTA (57.7) is actually lower than 4DHumans' (61.8). No code or checkpoints have been located as of 2026-07-05 despite CVPR 2026 acceptance, so RAM is not currently adoptable.

## 5. Diffusion pose priors and classical denoisers

**DPoser / DPoser-X** frame pose denoising/completion as an inverse problem solved via diffusion sampling. DPoser-X (ICCV 2025, MIT code) matches our MHR70 richness (body+hand+face). As a test-time regularizer on SMPLer-X output, it improves PA-MPJPE from 10.34mm to 7.33mm on ARCTIC. But its own limitations section states it "was not initially designed for temporal tasks" and treats each frame independently; on 40mm synthetic Gaussian noise, the body-only DPoser variant only reduces error to ~19.87mm MPJPE on AMASS — a partial, not complete, denoising, and roughly the same order of magnitude as our stated 2-8cm/frame raw noise floor. Both require the NC-licensed SMPL/SMPL-X/MANO/FLAME models.

**SmoothNet** (ECCV 2022) is a lighter, plug-and-play temporal-only refiner that could sit downstream of any pose estimator's output sequence, but it is 2021-era and was not benchmarked against 2024-2026 methods in this search (https://arxiv.org/abs/2112.13715).

## 6. Physics-based refinement

PhysPT (CVPR 2024) reduces acceleration error by 83.8% and foot-skating by 68.7% vs kinematic baselines using Euler-Lagrange/contact-force losses (https://arxiv.org/abs/2404.04430); PhysDiff (ICCV 2023) cuts physical error 86% on HumanML3D by projecting each diffusion step through a physics simulator; IPMAN (CVPR 2023) adds a Center-of-Pressure/Center-of-Mass stability term for floor contact. All of these numbers come from lab/generative-motion benchmarks (HumanML3D, AMASS-derived), not sports or elevated-camera footage, and no 2024-2026 successor was found that resolves the SMPL-license + hardcoded-20fps blockers that caused us to park PhysPT previously.

## 8. Small/far-subject and handheld robustness — direct evidence

A controlled study (2024) found MPJPE degrades from 33.27mm on "Large" person crops to 42.63mm on "Small" crops, attributing it to degraded 2D keypoint detection at low resolution plus the 3D model's difficulty reasoning about structure at small scale (https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10462-024-11019-3) — general supporting evidence for our own far/small-player finding, though not sports-specific. No paper in this search directly quantified small/far-subject degradation for SAM-3D-Body itself, nor for any of the video-native candidates. TRAM's masked-SLAM approach is the most concrete, ablated fix found for handheld drift specifically.

## Bottom line for the decision

The strongest, most directly comparable evidence (SMART, same architecture family, real broadcast sports video) supports incremental hardening of our existing classical pipeline over adopting a learned temporal net wholesale. The video-native replacement candidates (GVHMR, WHAM, TRAM, PromptHMR-Vid) show real, [VERIFIED]-confirmed gains on their own benchmarks, but none have been tested on footage resembling ours, all use a different mesh/skeleton format than MHR-70 (reintroducing our known mesh-skeleton-divergence bug class), and all carry non-trivial multi-person and licensing integration costs. The two papers built directly on frozen SAM-3D-Body (SAM-Body4D, the Dec-2025 retargeting paper) are the closest philosophical match to "improve without abandoning the ruling" but currently offer zero quantitative proof of benefit.


## Ranked options (synthesis agent; manager re-derives decisions)

### 1. A. Keep SAM-3D-Body per-frame; harden classical post-processing using SMART's validated recipe (RAFT+MAD camera tracking, foot-plane ground anchoring, MAD+Gaussian two-pass smoothing; explicitly skip learned temporal nets)
- **what:** No backbone change. Port specific, same-domain-validated post-processing techniques from SMART (near-identical architecture, real broadcast sports video) into our existing pipeline: RAFT-small optical flow + MAD outlier filtering for camera tracking (0.041deg rotation error at 55ms/frame, beating ECC homography's 0.107deg), per-track shape/scale locking, and confirmation that classical Gaussian/MAD smoothing beats a learned refinement net on held-out broadcast sports test data.
- **evidence:** SMART, arXiv:2605.31551 (2026): FIFA WorldPose val score 1.053->0.647, test Global MPJPE 0.324m/Local MPJPE 0.054m; explicitly REJECTED a learned temporal-refinement network because it degraded held-out performance vs plain classical smoothing, despite improving val by 22%.
- **expected_gain:** Moderate, low-risk improvement to camera-motion robustness (handheld drift) and world-grounding stability; validates that our classical-smoothing architecture choice is sound rather than a dead end.
- **cost:** Low -- reuses our existing pipeline shape, adds/swaps specific filter components.
- **risk:** Low -- SMART's own ablations show these techniques work on real broadcast sports footage, not just lab benchmarks.
- **confidence:** high

### 2. B. Add SAM-Body4D-style masklet-guided prompting as a frozen, training-free input-stabilization layer on top of SAM-3D-Body
- **what:** Feed SAM3 video-segmentation masklets (occlusion-aware, identity-consistent across frames) into SAM-3D-Body's encoder as mask prompts instead of independently-detected per-frame boxes/masks, without retraining SAM-3D-Body itself -- directly compatible with the owner's SAM-3D-only ruling since the backbone stays frozen.
- **evidence:** SAM-Body4D, arXiv:2512.08406 (Dec 2025): training-free, MIT-licensed code at github.com/gaomingqi/sam-body4d; reports ~2x batched-inference speedup but NO quantitative jitter/MPJPE metric anywhere -- evaluation is qualitative-only.
- **expected_gain:** Plausible reduction in input-driven jitter (noisy/inconsistent masks are a plausible contributor to our 2-8cm/frame noise), but entirely unverified numerically -- treat as an experiment, not a proven win.
- **cost:** Medium -- requires integrating SAM3 video segmentation + Diffusion-VAS occlusion inpainting into our pipeline; added GPU memory/runtime cost scales with occlusion.
- **risk:** Medium -- no benchmark exists to predict actual jitter reduction; could add latency without measurable benefit.
- **confidence:** low

### 3. C. Evaluate Fast SAM 3D Body as a drop-in accelerator to free GPU budget for more post-processing passes or higher far/small-player throughput
- **what:** Swap in the training-free Fast SAM 3D Body acceleration (parallelized crop extraction, pruned decoder, feedforward MHR->SMPL head) which keeps original 3DB weights frozen -- same owner-approved backbone, just faster.
- **evidence:** arXiv:2603.15603 (Mar 2026) [VERIFIED as accurately described, self-reported]: 8.3-10.9x speedup, ~65ms/frame on RTX 5090, MPJPE 54.8mm->59.5mm (worse) but PA-MPJPE 33.8mm->30.4mm (better) on 3DPW oracle protocol; MIT code at github.com/yangtiming/Fast-SAM-3D-Body.
- **expected_gain:** Could materially cut our stated ~8min/5-min-clip BODY-stage cost, freeing budget for more smoothing/multi-pass refinement especially for far/small players -- but no A100 numbers exist so actual gain on our hardware is unmeasured.
- **cost:** Low-medium -- integration + our own A100 benchmarking required since no A100 numbers are published.
- **risk:** Medium -- small accuracy regression on MPJPE (not PA-MPJPE) is real, and the paper's own jitter root-cause claim (self-occlusion during large rotations, fixed via multi-view fusion) is a robotics-teleop finding not validated against our sports-video jitter.
- **confidence:** medium

### 4. D. Use 'World-Coordinate Human Motion Retargeting via SAM 3D Body' (arXiv:2512.21573) purely as a design blueprint (not adoptable code) for shape-locking + latent-space smoothing + contact-aware ground optimization
- **what:** Borrow the architectural pattern (per-track beta/scale locking for constant bone length, Charbonnier-penalized latent-space smoothing with window-stitching, differentiable soft foot-contact GroundOptimizer solved via Adam) and reimplement analogous logic ourselves.
- **evidence:** arXiv:2512.21573 (Dec 2025): near-identical architecture to ours (frozen SAM-3D-Body/MHR -> tracking -> shape-lock -> latent smoothing -> contact-aware world grounding) but reports ZERO quantitative metrics and NO code release.
- **expected_gain:** Unknown magnitude -- directionally sound ideas (esp. per-track shape/scale locking, which is a plausible fix for shape-jitter we may already have) but cannot be benchmarked against our own numbers before building.
- **cost:** Medium -- full reimplementation from a paper with no released reference code.
- **risk:** Medium -- risk of investing engineering time on an idea with no proof of quantitative benefit.
- **confidence:** low

### 5. E. Swap SAM-3D-Body for a video-native world-grounded HMR model (GVHMR / WHAM / TRAM / PromptHMR-Vid) as the primary skeleton path -- the 'reopen the ruling' bet
- **what:** Replace our per-frame-model + classical-smoothing architecture with an end-to-end learned video model that already encodes temporal coherence and world grounding (GVHMR's gravity-view frame, WHAM's causal RNN, TRAM's masked-SLAM, or PromptHMR-Vid's full-image+prompt design).
- **evidence:** GVHMR [VERIFIED] beats WHAM on every world-grounded metric (RICH jitter 12.8 vs 19.7, EMDB foot-slide 3.5mm vs 4.4mm); PromptHMR-Vid reports EMDB-2 best-of-4 Jitter 16.3/Foot-Skate 3.5mm; TRAM cuts EMDB Root Translation Error to 3.1mm vs WHAM 8.4mm via masked-DROID-SLAM for handheld/moving cameras.
- **expected_gain:** Potentially the largest jitter/foot-slide/world-grounding improvement of any option, IF our sports-court footage (elevated/far/handheld, 4 players) behaves like 3DPW/EMDB/RICH pedestrian-scale lab benchmarks -- which is unverified since none of these methods were evaluated on anything resembling our footage.
- **cost:** High -- all output SMPL/SMPL-X (24-55 joint), not MHR-70+full mesh, creating exactly the mesh-skeleton-divergence bug class we already hit; all are single-person (need external multi-person tracking layered on top, none natively handle 4 simultaneous players); requires NC-licensed SMPL/SMPL-X body models (acceptable per our internal-R&D policy, but still a dependency); substantial engineering to re-plumb our whole tracking/court-placement/smoothing stack around a different skeleton format.
- **risk:** High -- unproven on far/small/elevated-camera sports footage; would require our own from-scratch benchmarking effort before any claim of 'measurably better' could reopen the owner's ruling.
- **confidence:** low

### 6. F. Add CoMotion (or wait for RAM's code release) as a multi-person occlusion-robust tracking layer, decoupled from the mesh/world-grounding stages
- **what:** Use CoMotion's tracking-by-attention (per-track recurrent hidden state through occlusion) purely for the person-tracking stage of our pipeline, keeping SAM-3D-Body for mesh/pose.
- **evidence:** CoMotion (ICLR 2025): MOTA 71.4/IDF1 79.5 on PoseTrack21 vs 4DHumans 56.7/70.9, 176ms/frame on V100; explicitly does not model camera motion, no shared world frame, and authors admit track collapse/ID switches in close-proximity scenes. RAM (CVPR 2026) shows further gains on identity-switch metrics (PoseTrack18 IDs=15 vs CoMotion 344) but has NO released code/checkpoints as of 2026-07-05.
- **expected_gain:** Could reduce our 4-player occlusion/identity-loss failures, a named weak point, but CoMotion's own admitted failure mode (close-proximity track collapse) is precisely our doubles/close-net scenario.
- **cost:** Medium -- integration as a tracking-only replacement; custom Apple Sample Code License (non-OSI) needs legal review even for internal use.
- **risk:** Medium-high -- CoMotion not validated at 4-person density either, and doesn't solve world-grounding/jitter, only identity persistence.
- **confidence:** low

### 7. G. Physics-based post-hoc refinement (PhysPT / PhysDiff / IPMAN) as a final polish pass
- **what:** Re-evaluate physics-aware refinement now that owner previously parked PhysPT (SMPL license + hardcoded 20fps).
- **evidence:** PhysPT (CVPR 2024): -83.8% acceleration error, -68.7% foot-skating vs kinematic baseline; PhysDiff (ICCV 2023): -86% physical-error on HumanML3D; IPMAN (CVPR 2023): CoM/CoP stability term for floor contact.

- **cost:** High -- no evidence the SMPL-license/hardcoded-fps blockers that caused us to park PhysPT have been resolved by any 2024-2026 successor found in this search.
- **risk:** High -- re-treads ground already parked by the owner without new evidence the blockers are lifted.

### 8. H. Bolt DPoser-X (whole-body diffusion pose prior) onto SAM-3D-Body's SMPL-X output as a per-frame regularizer
- **what:** Use DPoser-X's diffusion score model as a test-time optimization prior for per-frame pose plausibility/denoising, matching MHR70's body+hand+face richness.
- **evidence:** DPoser-X (ICCV 2025): PA-MPJPE improvements when used as SMPLer-X regularizer (10.34mm->7.33mm on ARCTIC); DPoser (body-only) reduces 40mm synthetic Gaussian noise to ~19.87mm MPJPE on AMASS.
